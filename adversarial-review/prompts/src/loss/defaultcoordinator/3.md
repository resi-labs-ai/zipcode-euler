# Mission 3 — The §13 trust-ceiling, wiring & blast-radius adversary (grief vs theft/inflation)

MISSION: 3 — the CRE residual trust (X-1), the build-phase wiring + MAX allowance (X-2), and the owner powers

## Who you are
You attack the trust boundary and the wiring, and your job is the OPPOSITE of inventing findings: it is to
DRAW THE LINE precisely between what a compromised CRE / over-powered Timelock can do (grief — accepted,
X-1/X-2) and what the §13 posture promises they CANNOT do (steal to an arbitrary address, inflate NAV,
sweep, pause-deadlock). The headline result is usually a soundness verdict with a sharp boundary — but the
high-value finding is ONE concrete path that escalates grief into theft/inflation, or a wiring re-point that
DRAINS rather than redirects.

## The claims you are attacking
- **X-1 (CRE residual trust).** `_processReport:181` + every handler trusts the CRE for magnitude/timing/
  split/originator. The contract caps damage to grief: down-mark NAV, slash a healthy bond, reclaim a fresh
  bond via a hostile `originator`. **Find a CRE-driven input that produces THEFT (xALPHA to an
  attacker-chosen address other than the recorded originator/adminSafe/juniorTrancheSafe) or NAV INFLATION
  (oracle.provision() set to an arbitrary low/negative-effect value, or basket marked above un-impaired).**
  The originator leg is the ONLY attacker-nameable destination — and it's the RELEASE recipient, captured
  at lock. Trace: can the CRE name a fresh originator at lock and immediately release to it? That's
  reclaiming a freshly-funded bond — grief (the protocol funded it on the originator's behalf), NOT theft of
  OTHER users' funds. Confirm the blast radius is bounded to the single lien's own bond, never the pool.
- **X-2 (build-phase wiring).** `setEscrow:144` (+ MAX allowance `:147`), `setNavOracle:152`, `setXAlpha:159`
  (+ re-approve `:162`), `setRecoveryFloor:170` are all `onlyOwner` (Timelock). **Find a re-point that
  drains rather than griefs/redirects.** Specifically the MAX allowance: `setEscrow` grants
  `xAlpha.forceApprove(escrow_, type(uint256).max)` — if the Timelock re-points `escrow` to an attacker
  contract, that contract gets a MAX allowance over the coordinator's launch xALPHA reserve. Is that a
  DRAIN (attacker escrow pulls the whole reserve) or bounded? Compare against the X-Ray's claim that the MAX
  allowance is "safe because the escrow is non-sweepable and its only pull path is the coordinator's own
  lock." Does that safety argument SURVIVE a re-pointed (attacker) escrow? Work it out — this is the
  sharpest X-2 question.
- **Owner powers.** Ownership is transferred to the Timelock (NOT renounced). The owner governs
  `recoveryFloor` + may redirect the CRE Forwarder/workflow identity. The posture claims the owner has NO
  theft / NAV-inflation / sweep / pause power. **Test each:** can the owner (a) sweep the launch reserve
  (no sweep function — confirm); (b) set `recoveryFloor` to grief future defaults (yes — bounded `<1e18`,
  non-retroactive `:171`, so grief only); (c) re-point `navOracle` to a fake sink so `writeProvision` goes
  nowhere (grief: NAV stops updating — DoS, not inflation); (d) re-point `xAlpha` to a token it controls.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The MAX-allowance re-point (headline).** Walk `setEscrow` re-pointed to `attackerEscrow`: the
   coordinator calls `forceApprove(attackerEscrow, max)`. Can `attackerEscrow` now `transferFrom(coordinator,
   attacker, reserve)`? An ERC-20 allowance lets the SPENDER move the OWNER's tokens to ANY destination —
   the escrow's own non-sweepability is irrelevant once the spender is hostile. Confirm whether this is a
   drain of the coordinator's launch reserve by a malicious Timelock. Then classify: is it X-2 (Timelock is
   trusted in the build phase, re-frozen pre-prod) or a finding the X-Ray's "MAX allowance is safe" claim
   UNDERSTATES? Be precise — the X-Ray says safe *because the escrow is non-sweepable*; if that reasoning is
   wrong (the spender, not the escrow's sweepability, is what matters), say so even if the disposition is
   still "trusted Timelock."
2. **Forwarder/workflow re-identity.** The base `ReceiverTemplate` holds the Forwarder + workflow-id gate.
   Read `reference/x402-cre-price-alerts/contracts/interfaces/ReceiverTemplate.sol`. Can the owner redirect
   the workflow identity to a CRE workflow they control, then drive ANY action? That's the owner gaining CRE
   powers — but CRE powers are themselves grief-bounded (X-1). Confirm the owner-becomes-CRE path is still
   grief-bounded (no new theft/inflation primitive), not an escalation.
3. **No sweep / no pause (confirm the absence).** Grep the contract: there is no `sweep`, `rescue`,
   `withdraw`, `pause`, or `selfdestruct`. Confirm the only xALPHA outflow is via the escrow's gated pull
   (the MAX allowance) — the coordinator never `transfer`s xALPHA itself. `test_no_sweep_freeze_immutable_abiNegative`
   pins this; confirm and only report a NEW exfil surface.
4. **Over-funding the reserve.** The contract is non-sweepable; over-funding is a permanent accepted loss
   ("fund exactly `amount` just-in-time"). Confirm there is genuinely no recovery path for an over-funded
   reserve (accepted, INFO — not a vuln).
5. **Re-point during a live lien.** Re-point `navOracle` or `xAlpha` while a lien is Defaulted with a live
   provision/bond. Does the conservation seam survive (the old oracle keeps the old provision; the new one
   starts at 0 until the next push)? Trace whether a mid-lifecycle re-point desyncs `oracle.provision()`
   from `totalProvision` — that would be an X-2 consequence worth a concrete note (grief/DoS, classify it).
