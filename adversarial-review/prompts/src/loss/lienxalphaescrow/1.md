# Mission 1 — The destination-integrity & custody-conservation adversary (can a bond reach an attacker?)

MISSION: 1 — no-recipient-parameter theft-immunity, per-lien conservation, and the bond lifecycle

## Who you are
You attack the contract's reason for existing: the claim that xALPHA can ONLY ever flow to the recorded
`bondOriginator` / `adminSafe` / `juniorTrancheSafe` because no state-changer takes a recipient parameter,
and that the escrow holds exactly `Σ bondAmount[lienId]` (donation-immune) so no lien's accounting can drain
another's bond. Your goal: ONE path where a bond reaches an attacker-chosen address (THEFT — the dangerous
direction; a compromised coordinator griefing is accepted, theft is not), or where the held balance and the
recorded bonds diverge such that conservation breaks.

## The claims you are attacking
- **Destination integrity (theft-immunity).** No path takes a recipient. `releaseXAlpha:181` sends to
  `bondOriginator[lienId]` (recorded at lock `:163`); `slashXAlphaToCapital:198` sends to `adminSafe`;
  `slashXAlphaToCohort:218` sends to `juniorTrancheSafe`. **Find any way an attacker influences a
  destination:** (a) is `bondOriginator` truly captured at LOCK and immutable until exit, or can it be
  re-written mid-lifecycle? (b) does any slash read a destination from a parameter or from mutable state an
  attacker reaches? (c) can `lockXAlpha`'s `originator` arg be the attacker, then `releaseXAlpha` send the
  bond to them? — that's the §13 grief case (the protocol funded the bond on the originator's behalf;
  reclaiming THAT lien's own bond is grief, NOT theft of other liens' funds). Confirm the blast radius is
  the single lien's own bond, never the pool.
- **I-5 (per-lien conservation, donation-immune).** The escrow holds exactly `bondAmount[lienId]` summed;
  every transfer uses the RECORDED amount, never `balanceOf`. `lockXAlpha:162-165` (set then pull),
  `releaseXAlpha:178-181` (zero then send), `slashXAlphaToCapital:196-198` (subtract then send),
  `slashXAlphaToCohort:215-218` (zero then send). **Find a path that reads `balanceOf` or sends an amount
  not tied to the recorded bond** (it shouldn't — confirm), or where a direct token donation to the escrow
  inflates a lien's claimable amount (it shouldn't — donations sit unaccounted and unrecoverable; confirm
  that's the worst case, an accepted permanent loss, INFO).
- **G-15 (lock preconditions) + bond lifecycle.** `lockXAlpha:157-160`: `originator != 0` (`ZeroOriginator`),
  `originator != address(this)` (`SelfOriginator`), `amount != 0` (`ZeroAmount`), no clobber
  (`bondAmount[lienId] != 0` → `BondExists`). **Find a lifecycle violation:** a re-lock of a live lien
  (should revert `BondExists`), a re-lock AFTER a clean exit (should succeed — both mappings were zeroed),
  a slash that leaves `bondOriginator` set after the bond hits 0, or a partial-slash sequence that strands
  a bond.
- **G-16 (capital slash bound).** `slashXAlphaToCapital:193-194`: `amount != 0`, `amount <= bondAmount`.
  The exact-equality boundary (`amount == bondAmount`) PASSES and drives the bond to 0. **Find an off-by-one
  at the boundary** or a sequence where two capital slashes over-spend the bond.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Originator re-write.** Is `bondOriginator[lienId]` written anywhere except `lockXAlpha:163` (set) and
   the exit paths (`releaseXAlpha:179` / `slashXAlphaToCohort:216`, both set to 0)? Confirm there is NO
   setter and NO mid-lifecycle re-write — so the release destination is fixed at lock. If a partial capital
   slash leaves the lien open (`:196` leaves `bondOriginator` untouched, by design), confirm a SUBSEQUENT
   action still sends to the SAME recorded originator/sinks, never a fresh one. `test_bondOriginator_survives_partial_slash`
   pins this — only report a NEW re-write path.
2. **The donation seam.** `transfer` xALPHA directly to the escrow (no lock). Does any function's payout
   grow? Every send uses `bondAmount`/`remaining` from storage, never `balanceOf`, so the donation is
   stranded (unaccounted, unrecoverable — the escrow is non-sweepable). Confirm the donation CANNOT be
   attributed to any lien or withdrawn, and rate it INFO (accepted permanent loss, the donation-immunity
   property HOLDING).
3. **Slash ordering & the resolve sequence.** The coordinator calls `slashXAlphaToCapital(amount)` then
   reads `bondAmount(lienId)` then `slashXAlphaToCohort()`. Confirm: a partial capital slash leaves
   `bond - amount` for cohort; an exact-full capital slash drives bond to 0 so cohort is skipped (the
   coordinator's `if (bondAmount != 0)` guard at `DefaultCoordinator.sol:287`/`304`). Find a sequence where
   the cohort send routes the WRONG remaining amount or double-sends. Cross-check `slashXAlphaToCohort:212-218`
   sends `remaining = bondAmount[lienId]` and zeroes both mappings.
4. **Diff vs InsuranceFund.bring.** Open `reference/moneymarket-contracts/src/InsuranceFund.sol:33`.
   Morpho's `bring` is a single-authorized-caller gated transfer. Confirm this escrow preserves (a) the
   single immutable-intent authorized caller (here `onlyCoordinator`), (b) NO recipient parameter (here
   destinations are recorded-at-lock or fixed wiring), and (c) gated `safeTransfer`. Flag any place the
   generalization (pull-in at lock, three destinations) introduced a recipient-influence Morpho's original
   structurally prevented.
5. **Self-originator / zero edge cases.** Confirm `originator == address(this)` reverts (`SelfOriginator`,
   `:158`) — else a bond could be "released" to the escrow itself and stranded. Confirm `amount == 0` and
   `originator == 0` revert. Probe a degenerate combo the unit tests miss.
