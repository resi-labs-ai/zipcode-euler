# Mission 4 — The burnFor & wiring adversary

MISSION: 4 — burnFor authority + atomicity (I-9), and the build-phase wiring blast radius

## Who you are
You attack the exit executor and the configuration surface: the claim that `burnFor` is windowController-only,
pure supply reduction (no asset payout), atomic on an under-funded engine, and that the 8 `onlyOwner` setters
(incl. `setBaal`, which re-derives loot/juniorTrancheSafe) redirect at worst rather than drain. Your goal: ONE
path where a non-windowController burns, where `burnFor` moves a basket asset (it shouldn't — pure supply
reduction), where an under-funded burn leaks, or where a re-point DRAINS / breaks conservation.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-9 (`burnFor` windowController-only, pure supply reduction).** `burnFor` (`:200`) requires `msg.sender ==
  windowController` (`:201`, `NotWindowController`), `juniorTrancheEngine != 0` (`:202`, `NotWired`), `amount != 0`
  (`:203`). It burns the Gate's Loot (`:204`) + the engine Safe's szipUSD (`:205`) — NO asset payout (the basket
  is untouched). **Find a path where a non-windowController burns, where `burnFor` moves a basket asset, or where
  it burns szipUSD the engine doesn't hold without reverting** (the under-funded case must roll back atomically).
- **The under-funded engine rollback.** `SzipUSD.burn(juniorTrancheEngine, amount)` (`:205`) reverts if the
  engine holds < `amount` szipUSD. Since `burnLoot` (`:204`) ran first, **confirm the szipUSD-burn revert unwinds
  the `burnLoot`** (same tx, atomic) — so a burn for more than the engine holds leaves supply + Loot intact (the
  X-Ray's `test_burnFor_reverts_when_engine_underfunded`).
- **The wiring blast radius (8 `onlyOwner` setters).** `setShareToken`, `setWindowController`,
  `setJuniorTrancheEngine`, `setBaal` (re-derives loot/juniorTrancheSafe, `:115`), `setNavOracle`, `setTokens`,
  `setTvlCap`, plus the ctor. **Pressure-test:** `setWindowController` (`:101`) changes WHO can burn;
  `setShareToken` (`:94`) changes WHICH token is minted/burned; `setBaal` (`:115`) swaps the Loot custodian base.
  Which re-point DRAINS or breaks conservation vs merely redirects future behavior?

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The burnFor authority.** Confirm ONLY `windowController` can call `burnFor` (`:201`) — not the owner, not a
   depositor, not the engine. The windowController is the CRE keeper. A burn reduces supply (NAV-per-share ticks
   up for stayers) — a malicious windowController could burn the engine's szipUSD at will, but that's pure supply
   reduction matched by Loot burn (conservation holds) and bounded by what the engine holds. Frame: is a rogue
   windowController a theft (no — no asset moves) or a NAV-manipulation grief? Rate.
2. **`setShareToken` mid-life desync.** If the Timelock re-points `shareToken` (`:94`) to a DIFFERENT token after
   szipUSD has been minted, the conservation identity breaks: `loot.balanceOf(gate)` tracks the OLD token's
   supply, but new mints/burns hit the NEW token. Trace whether this is a reachable conservation break or a
   governance footgun (owner-only, pre-prod re-freeze). The X-Ray flags the build-phase wiring as the standing
   residual — confirm a `setShareToken` swap is the sharpest conservation hazard among the setters.
3. **`setBaal` re-derivation.** `setBaal` (`:115`) sets `baal`, `loot = lootToken()`, `juniorTrancheSafe =
   avatar()`. After a re-point, `loot.balanceOf(gate)` reads the NEW Loot — but the OLD Loot the Gate holds (from
   prior deposits) is now off-book, and `totalSupply` (szipUSD, unchanged) no longer matches the new
   `loot.balanceOf(gate)`. Is that a reachable conservation break (owner-only)? And does re-pointing
   `juniorTrancheSafe` redirect where NEW deposits land (`:170`) — a redirect of future deposits, or a drain of
   existing basket value (no — existing assets stay in the old Safe)?
4. **`NotWired` paths.** `depositFor` reverts `NotWired` if `shareToken == 0` (`:159`); `burnFor` if
   `juniorTrancheEngine == 0` (`:202`). The X-Ray notes these revert paths aren't directly tested. Confirm a
   fresh Gate (pre-wiring) fails closed on both flows rather than minting/burning against a zero address.
5. **ctor zero-guards.** The constructor (`:76`) zero-guards baal/navOracle/zipUSD/xAlpha and rejects `tvlCap ==
   0` (`:82`). The X-Ray notes the negative ctor paths aren't separately exercised — confirm by reading they
   revert `ZeroAddress`/`ZeroAmount`.
