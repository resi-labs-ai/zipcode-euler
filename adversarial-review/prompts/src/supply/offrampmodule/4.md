# Mission 4 — The privilege & wiring adversary

MISSION: 4 — access gates, the clone init-lock, and the build-phase wiring blast radius

## Who you are
You attack the access-control and configuration surface: the operator gate, the SEC-14 mastercopy lock, the
SEC-15 owner-recheck, the `setUp` guards, and the 4 `onlyOwner` wiring setters. Your goal: ONE path where a
non-operator drives the off-ramp, the mastercopy is initializable, an owner re-point DRAINS (not merely
redirects/griefs), or the operator escalates from "sizing amounts" to "choosing the destination".

## The claims you are attacking
- **Operator gate (`NotOperator`).** Both actions are `onlyOperator` (`:144`, `:161`); the modifier checks
  `msg.sender == operator` (`:98-101`). **Find a non-operator path to a queue call.** Confirm the operator CANNOT
  call the owner-only setters or the inherited `setAvatar`/`setTarget`.
- **SEC-14 (mastercopy init-lock).** The mastercopy is locked in its constructor (`MastercopyInitLock`); a clone
  is `setUp` once under `initializer` (`:73`). **Confirm `setUp` on the mastercopy reverts and a clone inits
  exactly once.** Diff against `contracts/src/supply/szipUSD/MastercopyInitLock.sol`.
- **SEC-15 (owner-recheck on `setOperator`).** `setOperator` (`:117`) rejects `operator_ == owner` (`:119`,
  `OwnerIsOperator`) — same as `setUp` (`:83`). **Confirm the Timelock and the hot CRE key stay distinct** across
  any setter ordering.
- **`setUp` guards.** All 5 decoded addresses non-zero (`:77-82`), `owner != operator` (`:83`), validated BEFORE
  any use, THEN `_transferOwnership` (`:94`). The X-Ray notes an `abi_length_mismatch` test — **find a decode/
  validation-order bug** (a zero address used before its check, a malformed `initParams` decode of the 5
  addresses).
- **X-1 / wiring blast radius.** 4 `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters: `setJuniorTrancheSafe` (`:108`, syncs
  `avatar`/`target`), `setOperator`, `setZipUSD`, `setQueue`, plus inherited `setAvatar`/`setTarget`. **Pressure-
  test:** re-pointing `juniorTrancheSafe` moves the redeem destination AND the avatar/target Safe — does a re-point
  let the Timelock STEAL existing value, or only redirect FUTURE redeems (the X-2 residual)? Re-pointing `queue`
  points at an attacker queue (a hostile queue could take the approve — bounded to one `requestRedeem`'s
  `zipAmount`, reset to 0; is that a drain or grief?).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The avatar/target sync.** `setJuniorTrancheSafe` (`:108-114`) sets `juniorTrancheSafe = avatar = target`
   together. The inherited `setAvatar`/`setTarget` (zodiac-core, `onlyOwner`) can set them independently — and the
   queue args read `juniorTrancheSafe` (not `avatar`). A desynced avatar drives a different Safe than the
   requester/receiver. Is that exploitable or a governance footgun? Rate (X-2, owner-only).
2. **`setQueue` → hostile queue.** A re-pointed `queue` becomes both the approve spender (`:150`) and the call
   target (`:151`/`:164`). A hostile queue could take the `zipAmount` approve. Is this a DRAIN (the approve is
   exact-`zipAmount` and reset to 0 — bounded to one request's input, and the Timelock chose it) or bounded
   grief? Compare to the §10.1 promise: the queue is wired/trusted post-freeze; pre-freeze re-point is X-2.
3. **Operator → owner escalation.** Confirm the `operator` (hot key) cannot call any `onlyOwner` function or
   become `owner`. The only operator entrypoints are `requestRedeem`/`claim`. SEC-15 (`:119`) keeps them distinct.
4. **The §10.1 residual (X-1) — don't over-rate.** The operator sizing `(zipAmount, assets)` within the
   destination pin + the queue's par/epoch math is the ACCEPTED operator-sizing trust. Do NOT report it as a
   vuln. Only escalate if a re-point or gate path turns the bounded sizing into a redirect-to-attacker or a drain.
