# Mission 1 — The redirect adversary (can redeemed USDC leave the rq Safe, or C4 break?)

MISSION: 1 — destination integrity + the C4 authorization (the §10.1 boundary)

## Who you are
You attack the whole safety story of a pure off-ramp driver: every queue arg is the pinned `juniorTrancheSafe`,
never operator-supplied, so redeemed USDC can accrue NOWHERE but the rq Safe. Your goal: ONE path where the
`requester`/`owner`/`receiver` queue args become operator-influenced, where the redeemed USDC lands off the rq
Safe, or where the C4 authorization (`queue sees the Safe as msg.sender`) breaks.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (destination integrity).** `requestRedeem` builds `queue.requestRedeem(zipAmount, juniorTrancheSafe,
  juniorTrancheSafe)` (`:151`) and `claim` builds `queue.withdraw(assets, juniorTrancheSafe, juniorTrancheSafe)`
  (`:164`) — every address arg is the set-once `juniorTrancheSafe` (cached locally as `rq`, `:149`/`:163`),
  re-pointable only by `onlyOwner` (`setJuniorTrancheSafe:108`). **Find any path where the operator can set a
  queue arg to a non-Safe address.** The operator's ONLY inputs are `(zipAmount)` and `(assets)` — confirm
  neither reaches an address arg.
- **C4 authorization (the §10.1 trust).** The queue's `requestRedeem(shares, requester, owner)` (`:15`) and
  `withdraw(assets, receiver, requester)` (`:16`) positional args matter. Because the module `exec`s THROUGH the
  Safe, the queue sees `msg.sender == juniorTrancheSafe`, so `requester == owner == juniorTrancheSafe` satisfies
  the queue's `owner == msg.sender` check. **Confirm the positional args are correct** (the X-Ray's `:13-17`
  comment documents `requestRedeem(shares, requester, owner)` and `withdraw(assets, receiver, requester)` — verify
  the call sites `:151`/`:164` pass `juniorTrancheSafe` in the right positions so the claim accrues to the rq
  Safe, not a wrong party). A swapped arg could route the claim or fail the auth.
- **Never touches the warehouse Safe / other legs.** The X-Ray asserts the off-ramp NEVER drives the warehouse
  Safe (REDEEM/REPAY is `WarehouseAdminModule`'s job) and never sells xALPHA. Confirm there is no path in this
  contract to any Safe but `juniorTrancheSafe` and no token but `zipUSD`/the queue's USDC.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Trace the operator arguments.** `zipAmount` → `requestRedeem` arg 1 (`:151`); `assets` → `withdraw` arg 1
   (`:164`). Confirm NEITHER reaches args 2/3 (the addresses). Both calls use TYPED `encodeCall` (`:151`, `:164`)
   — a positional regression would fail to compile against the interface; confirm the interface signature matches
   the queue's actual one (`:13-17`).
2. **The approve target/spender.** The two approves in `requestRedeem` (`:150`, `:152`) call `zipUSD.approve(q,
   …)` where `q = queue` (`:148`). Confirm the spender is the wired queue and the token is the wired `zipUSD` —
   not operator-chosen.
3. **Positional-arg correctness (the subtle one).** `requestRedeem(zipAmount, rq, rq)` maps to `(shares,
   requester, owner)`; `withdraw(assets, rq, rq)` maps to `(assets, receiver, requester)`. Confirm passing `rq`
   in BOTH address slots is correct for the queue's semantics — i.e. the rq Safe is simultaneously
   requester/owner/receiver, and the C4 `owner == msg.sender` holds. Find any queue semantic where `receiver !=
   requester` matters and the module gets it wrong.
4. **`value == 0` / no passthrough.** `_exec` (`:173`) drives `execAndReturnData(to, 0, data, Operation.Call)` —
   `value` literal 0, `Operation.Call`, `to` only ever `zipUSD` or `queue`. Confirm no native-value send, no
   delegatecall, no generic passthrough.
