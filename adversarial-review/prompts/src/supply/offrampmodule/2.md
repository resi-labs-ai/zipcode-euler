# Mission 2 — The unit-desync & par-neutrality adversary

MISSION: 2 — the whole-unit guard vs LIVE `scaleUp()` (I-2) and par NAV-neutrality (I-5)

## Who you are
You attack the one piece of arithmetic the driver owns: the whole-unit check on `requestRedeem`, and the
economic claim that a full redeem→claim cycle is par-neutral. Your goal: ONE path where a `zipAmount` that is
NOT a whole multiple of the queue's live `scaleUp()` slips through (so the queue mis-accounts a fractional unit),
where the unit check reads a stale/wrong scale, or where the cycle is not value-neutral because the driver feeds
the queue wrong.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-2 (whole-unit vs LIVE `scaleUp()`).** `requestRedeem` reverts `NotWholeUnit` unless `zipAmount %
  IZipRedemptionQueue(queue).scaleUp() == 0` (`:146`) — read LIVE off the queue every call, NEVER a hard-coded
  `1e12`. **Find a path where a non-whole amount passes**, or where the check reads a stale/hard-coded scale. Why
  it matters: `scaleUp()` is mutable on the queue (re-derived on `setTokens`); a hard-coded unit would desync if
  the queue re-scales. Confirm the modulo is against the live read and that `scaleUp() == 0` (a queue
  mis-config) would revert (div-by-zero in `%`) rather than silently pass.
- **I-5 (par NAV-neutrality).** Over a full cycle the basket loses `Q` zipUSD ($1) and gains `par` USDC ($1) —
  exactly neutral. This is the queue's economic guarantee (par redemption), driven by this module. **The driver's
  only job is to feed whole units and pin destinations** — confirm the module does NOT introduce any rounding,
  fee, or scaling of its own that would break neutrality (it passes `zipAmount`/`assets` straight through). A
  finding here is only valid if the DRIVER (not the queue) breaks neutrality.
- **The modulo edge.** `zipAmount == 0` reverts `ZeroAmount` (`:145`) BEFORE the modulo. A `zipAmount` that is a
  multiple of `1e12` but NOT of the live `scaleUp()` (if scaleUp != 1e12) must revert `NotWholeUnit` — the X-Ray
  proves this with a non-1e12 scaleUp. **Find a scaleUp value where the modulo behaves unexpectedly** (e.g.
  `scaleUp == 1` makes every amount "whole" — is that a valid queue state, and is it safe? It would mean no
  whole-unit constraint, which may be correct for an 18-dp share).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Order of guards.** `:145` (`ZeroAmount`) runs before `:146` (the modulo). Confirm a zero amount can't reach
   the `% scaleUp()` div. Then confirm `scaleUp() == 0` (queue not yet `setTokens`-configured) reverts the modulo
   (Solidity `%` by zero reverts) — fail-closed, not a silent pass. Rate: a zero scaleUp is a queue mis-config
   (the queue's concern), but confirm our driver fails closed against it.
2. **Stale-scale resistance.** The check re-reads `scaleUp()` every call (`:146`). Confirm there is NO cached/
   stored unit anywhere in the module and no path that uses a hard-coded `1e12`. A re-scale on the queue between
   two `requestRedeem` calls must be picked up automatically.
3. **`claim` has no unit guard — is that right?** `claim(assets)` (`:161`) only guards `ZeroAmount` (`:162`), no
   whole-unit check. Confirm that's correct: `assets` is USDC (the realized fill), not shares, so a whole-unit
   constraint wouldn't apply. Find any case where an arbitrary `assets` value mis-claims (the queue bounds it to
   the realized fill — confirm the queue, not the module, owns that).
4. **Driver-introduced rounding.** Trace `zipAmount` and `assets` from the entrypoint to the queue call — confirm
   NO arithmetic transforms them (no scaling, no fee deduction, no division). The module passes them verbatim.
   Any transform would break par-neutrality; confirm there is none.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_requestRedeem_reads_scaleUp_live` (sets a non-1e12 scaleUp; an amount that's a multiple of 1e12 but not the
live scaleUp reverts `NotWholeUnit`), `test_fork_full_cycle_par_nav_neutral` (`zipUSD out == USDC in at par`
against the real queue + settled epoch), `test_requestRedeem_zero_amount_reverts`, `test_claim_zero_amount_reverts`.
Confirming the unit guard is live + the cycle is par-neutral goes in your Summary. A finding must be a concrete
unit-desync / driver-introduced-rounding path the live-scaleUp + fork-cycle tests do NOT cover — NOT a restatement
of the queue's own par/epoch logic (out of scope; tested in the queue's suite).
