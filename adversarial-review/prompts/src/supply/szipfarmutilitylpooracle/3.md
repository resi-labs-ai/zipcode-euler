# Mission 3 ‚Äî The wiring adversary (Timelock setters, scale re-derive, ctor)

MISSION: 3 ‚Äî Timelock setters, scale re-derivation, ctor zero-guards & wiring residual

## Who you are
You attack the build-phase mutable wiring: the three `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters and the constructor.
Wiring is settable (not immutable) by design until the pre-prod freeze ‚Äî but a setter that silently
mis-scales every quote, or a ctor that admits a degenerate config, breaks the oracle without any malicious
mark. Your goal: find a setter or ctor path that corrupts the scale/key/window such that subsequent quotes
are wrong (most dangerously, over-valued).

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-7 (setters onlyOwner + zero-guard + effect).** `setQuote(quote_)` re-derives `scale`; `setLpToken`
  re-points the priced key; `setValidityWindow` tightens the staleness window. **The dangerous one is
  `setQuote`:** `scale = calcScale(18, quoteDecimals, quoteDecimals)` ‚Äî re-pointing to a quote token with
  DIFFERENT decimals than the original silently re-scales EVERY future quote. Confirm the re-derive reads the
  NEW token's `decimals()` (not a cached value), that `setQuote`/`setLpToken` zero-guard, and that the old
  pair stops pricing (`NotSupported`) after a re-point.
- **I-8 (ctor zero-guards).** `constructor(forwarder, quote_, validityWindow_, lpToken_)` ‚Äî zero
  `quote`/`lpToken` ‚Üí `ZeroAddress`; zero forwarder ‚Üí `InvalidForwarderAddress` (parent). Confirm `scale` is
  derived correctly at construction and that nothing else is left in a fail-open default.
- **X-3 (build-phase mutable wiring residual).** The Timelock can re-point all wiring; that's the documented
  subsystem residual, closed by the pre-prod immutable re-freeze. **A bare re-point restatement is INFO** ‚Äî
  the sharp finding is a re-point that DRAINS or breaks the scale/liveness invariant (a hostile or
  fat-fingered Timelock re-point that mis-scales without reverting), not "the Timelock is trusted."

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Scale re-derive staleness.** Walk `setQuote`: does it re-read `IERC20Metadata(quote_).decimals()` and
   recompute `scale`, or could it reuse a stale decimals/scale? Construct: original quote = USDC (6dp),
   re-point to an 18dp quote ‚Äî does `scale` change correctly, and does an un-re-derived scale over- or
   under-value? A mis-scale here mis-prices every borrow silently (no revert).
2. **`setValidityWindow` no zero-guard.** The X-Ray notes there is intentionally no zero-guard (0 ‚áí every
   read past `ts` fails closed). Confirm `setValidityWindow(0)` is a safe fail-closed state, not a fail-open
   one ‚Äî and that a very large window (loosening staleness to near-infinite) is the only "dangerous"
   direction, bounded by Timelock trust.
3. **Re-point old-pair leak.** After `setLpToken(new)` or `setQuote(new)`, confirm the OLD `(lpToken, quote)`
   pair reverts `NotSupported` and cannot still be priced from a stale cache (a router still wired to the old
   pair must fail closed, not serve a stale mark).
4. **ctor zero / scale.** Zero quote, zero lpToken, zero forwarder each revert; non-zero derives a correct
   `scale`. Any arg left unguarded that yields a fail-open oracle?
5. **Diff vs the precedent's setter discipline.** Compare the three setters against `SzAlphaRateOracle` /
   `ZipcodeOracleRegistry`'s wiring setters ‚Äî same `onlyOwner` + zero-guard + re-derive shape? Any guard the
   precedent has that this one dropped (the recurring subsystem setter-gap class)?

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_setQuote_guards_and_effect` / `test_setLpToken_guards_and_effect` / `test_setValidityWindow_guards_and_effect`
(each: non-owner revert + zero-guard where applicable + re-point/tighten effect, old pair reverts), and
`test_ctor_zeroQuote_reverts` / `_zeroLpToken_reverts` / `_zeroForwarder_reverts` (I-8). Confirming these hold
goes in the Summary. A finding must be a concrete scale-corruption or fail-open re-point these tests don't
cover ‚Äî most valuably a `setQuote`-to-different-decimals mis-scale, or a re-point that leaves a stale cache
priceable.
