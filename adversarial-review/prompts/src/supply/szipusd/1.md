# Mission 1 — The supply-control adversary (can a non-Gate mint or burn?)

MISSION: 1 — Gate-only mint/burn (I-1) + the supply-conservation it underpins (I-2) + the vanilla-ERC20 claim (I-3)

## Who you are
You attack the only surface on this token that matters: supply control. Mint and burn must be callable ONLY by
the wired Gate, and the token must add NOTHING to the OZ ERC-20 surface that could move balances outside that
control (no hook, fee, rebase, or pause). Your goal: ONE path where a non-Gate caller mints or burns, or where a
hidden modification to the OZ surface lets balances change outside the Gate.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (mint/burn are Gate-only).** `mint` (`:37`) and `burn` (`:43`) both check `msg.sender != gate` → `NotGate`
  (`:38`, `:44`) before `_mint`/`_burn`. **Find a non-Gate path to `_mint`/`_burn`** — another function that calls
  them, an inherited OZ function exposed, or a `gate` that could be set to an attacker (mission 2's `setGate`). The
  operator/any user must NOT be able to mint or burn.
- **I-2 (supply pairs 1:1 with the Gate's Loot).** The conservation `totalSupply() == loot.balanceOf(gate)` is the
  GATE's invariant (each szipUSD mint/burn paired with a Gate `mintLoot`/`burnLoot`). **This token's role is only
  to make the Gate the SOLE supply mover** — confirm there's no path that changes `totalSupply` without the Gate
  (which would desync the conservation the Gate maintains). A non-Gate mint/burn would break it.
- **I-3 (non-rebasing, freely transferable, unmodified OZ).** Everything but mint/burn/setGate is stock OZ. **Find
  ANY hidden override** — a `_update`/`_beforeTokenTransfer`/`_afterTokenTransfer` hook, a fee-on-transfer, a
  rebase, a pause, a blocklist, a transfer hook — that the "vanilla" claim denies. Read the whole contract: it
  should be exactly `ERC20` + `Ownable` + the 3 additions, nothing else.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Enumerate every state-changing path.** The only functions are the ctor, `setGate`, `mint`, `burn` (+
   inherited OZ transfer/approve). `mint`/`burn` are `onlyGate` (the inline `msg.sender != gate` check). Confirm
   there is NO other path to `_mint`/`_burn` — no inherited OZ function that mints (OZ `ERC20` has no public mint),
   no `_update` override that could mint/burn.
2. **The `onlyGate` check robustness.** `if (msg.sender != gate) revert NotGate()` (`:38`, `:44`). Confirm `gate`
   is the wired Gate (set in ctor, re-pointable only by `setGate` onlyOwner — mission 2). A non-Gate `msg.sender`
   always reverts. No operator role exists on this token (confirm — the X-Ray says "no CRE operator").
3. **The vanilla claim — read for hidden hooks.** Confirm the contract is `contract SzipUSD is ERC20, Ownable`
   (`:14`) with NO `_update` override, no `_beforeTokenTransfer`, no fee logic, no rebase, no pause. The ONLY
   overrides/additions are `mint`/`burn`/`setGate`/ctor. A hidden transfer hook would mean balances move
   differently than stock OZ — confirm there is none.
4. **The two-token tie.** Confirm a `burn(from, amount)` (`:43`) burns from an arbitrary `from` (Gate-supplied —
   the engine Safe in practice) WITHOUT requiring `from`'s approval (it's `_burn`, not `burnFrom`). Is that safe?
   Yes — only the Gate can call it, and the Gate burns the engine Safe's szipUSD it controls. Confirm a non-Gate
   can't burn an arbitrary holder's tokens (the `onlyGate` gate prevents it). A burn-without-approval reachable by
   a non-Gate would be a theft of holders' tokens.
