# Mission 2 — The setGate & ctor adversary (can the minter/burner be hijacked?)

MISSION: 2 — `setGate` onlyOwner + zero-guard + effect, and the ctor zero-guard (I-4)

## Who you are
You attack the re-point of WHO controls the token's supply: `setGate` changes the sole minter/burner. Your goal:
ONE path where a non-owner re-points `gate` (hijacking mint/burn), where a zero gate bricks or opens supply
control, or where the constructor admits a zero gate.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-4 (`setGate` onlyOwner + zero-guard + effect; ctor zero-guard).** `setGate` (`:30`) is `onlyOwner` and
  reverts `ZeroAddress` on zero (`:31`); after a re-point the NEW gate can mint and the OLD gate reverts `NotGate`.
  The ctor (`:23`) rejects a zero `gate_` (`:24`). **Find a path where a non-owner calls `setGate`** (hijacking the
  minter — an attacker gate could then mint unbacked szipUSD, breaking the Gate's conservation), or where a zero
  gate slips through (a zero gate would make `mint`/`burn` uncallable — `msg.sender != address(0)` always — i.e.
  brick supply changes; the zero-guard prevents it).
- **The owner is the Timelock.** `Ownable(msg.sender)` in the ctor (`:23`) sets the deployer as owner; build-phase
  it's transferred to the Timelock. `setGate` is `onlyOwner` (the Timelock). **Confirm the owner can't be the
  Gate or an operator** — and that a `setGate` re-point is a deliberate Timelock act (the documented build-phase
  residual), not reachable by mint/burn callers.
- **The re-point conservation hazard.** If the Timelock re-points `gate` to a NEW ExitGate while the OLD Gate
  holds Loot matching the existing `totalSupply`, the conservation `totalSupply == loot.balanceOf(gate)` now reads
  against the NEW gate (which holds no Loot yet). **Is this token responsible for that desync, or the Gate?** The
  token just enforces "only the current gate mints/burns"; the conservation across a Gate swap is the Gate's/
  operations' concern (the `exitgate` mission 4 covers `setBaal`/`setShareToken`). Note the seam but attribute it
  correctly — this token's `setGate` correctly transfers mint authority; it doesn't migrate balances (nor should
  it).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **`setGate` auth + zero-guard.** Confirm `setGate` (`:30`) carries `onlyOwner` and `if (gate_ == address(0))
   revert ZeroAddress()` (`:31`). A non-owner → `OwnableUnauthorizedAccount`; a zero → `ZeroAddress`. The X-Ray's
   `test_szipUSD_setGate_and_ctor_zero_guard` proves all of this incl. the old-gate-loses-mint effect.
2. **The ctor zero-guard.** `constructor(address gate_)` (`:23`) reverts `ZeroAddress` on a zero `gate_` (`:24`).
   Confirm a Gate-less token can't be deployed (which would brick mint/burn from the start).
3. **Effect of a re-point.** After `setGate(newGate)`, confirm `mint`/`burn` accept `newGate` and reject the old
   gate (the `gate` storage is updated, `:32`). Find any caching of the old gate (there's none — `mint`/`burn`
   read `gate` live).
4. **No operator/other privilege.** Confirm there is NO operator, pause, or other privileged role — only `owner`
   (setGate) and `gate` (mint/burn). The attack surface is exactly these two. A hidden admin would be a finding.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_szipUSD_setGate_and_ctor_zero_guard` — ctor rejects zero; `setGate` non-owner → `OwnableUnauthorizedAccount`,
zero → `ZeroAddress`, re-point takes effect, the NEW gate can mint while the OLD gate reverts `NotGate`. Confirming
`setGate`/ctor guards hold goes in your Summary. A finding must be a concrete non-owner-re-point / zero-gate /
hidden-privilege path the setGate+ctor test does NOT cover — a restatement of "the Timelock can re-point the gate"
is INFO (build-phase residual); the Gate-swap conservation seam belongs to the `exitgate` drill, not here.
