# Mission 4 — The privilege & wiring adversary

MISSION: 4 — access gates, the clone init-lock, the coverage-gate setter, and the build-phase wiring blast radius

## Who you are
You attack the access-control and configuration surface: the operator gate, the SEC-14 mastercopy lock, the
SEC-15 owner-recheck, the `setUp` guards, and the 7 `onlyOwner` wiring setters (including the load-bearing
`setCoverageGate`). Your goal: ONE path where a non-operator drives the LP lifecycle, the mastercopy is
initializable, the coverage gate is silently disabled by a non-owner, or an owner re-point DRAINS rather than
redirects.

## The claims you are attacking
- **Operator gate (`NotOperator`).** All 4 mutators are `onlyOperator` (`:217`, `:261`, `:278`, `:288`); the
  modifier checks `msg.sender == operator` (`:182-185`). **Find a non-operator path to a mutator.** Confirm the
  operator CANNOT call any `onlyOwner` setter (especially `setCoverageGate`) or the inherited `setAvatar`/
  `setTarget`.
- **SEC-14 (mastercopy init-lock).** The mastercopy is locked in its constructor (`MastercopyInitLock`); a clone
  is `setUp` once under `initializer` (`:85`). **Confirm `setUp` on the mastercopy reverts and a clone inits
  exactly once.** Diff against `contracts/src/supply/szipUSD/MastercopyInitLock.sol`.
- **SEC-15 (owner-recheck on `setOperator`).** `setOperator` (`:139`) rejects `operator_ == owner` (`:141`).
  **Confirm the Timelock and the hot CRE key stay distinct** across any setter ordering.
- **`setUp` guards + the coverageGate exception.** Five addresses non-zero (`:95-98`) BUT `coverageGate_` MAY be
  zero (`:100`, gate OFF) — validated before use, THEN the live token-leg read (`:113-115`), THEN
  `_transferOwnership` (`:120`). **Find a decode/validation-order bug** (a zero `ichiVault` reaching the live
  `token0()` staticcall before its check — the X-Ray notes `_zero_ichiVault_at_guard_not_staticcall`).
- **X-1/X-2 / wiring blast radius — `setCoverageGate` is the key one.** 7 `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters:
  `setJuniorTrancheEngine` (`:130`, syncs avatar/target), `setOperator`, `setIchiVault`, `setGauge`, `setToken0`,
  `setToken1`, and **`setCoverageGate`** (`:176`, where ZERO = gate OFF). **Pressure-test:** can `setCoverageGate(0)`
  silently disable the path-lock — and who can call it (only the Timelock — so it's the X-2 residual, but flag
  the consequence: a disabled gate makes `removeLiquidity` unconstrained). Does any re-point let the Timelock
  STEAL existing value, or only redirect/disable FUTURE behavior?

## Specific attacks to run
1. **`setCoverageGate(0)` consequence.** `setCoverageGate` (`:176`) accepts `address(0)` (`:177`, "valid gate off
   value"). Confirm ONLY the Timelock can call it (`onlyOwner`) and the operator cannot. Frame the risk: a
   Timelock that zeroes the gate turns off the LP-dissolution path-lock entirely — a deliberate governance act
   (X-2), but note it as the single highest-consequence wiring re-point. There is no operator path to it.
2. **The avatar/target sync.** `setJuniorTrancheEngine` (`:130-136`) sets `juniorTrancheEngine = avatar = target`
   together. The inherited `setAvatar`/`setTarget` (zodiac-core, `onlyOwner`) can set them independently — and
   the deposit `to`/balance reads use `juniorTrancheEngine` (not `avatar`). A desynced avatar drives a different
   Safe than the recipient. Exploitable or governance footgun? Rate (X-2, owner-only).
3. **`setIchiVault`/`setGauge` re-point.** A re-pointed `ichiVault` becomes the deposit target + approve spender
   (and the LP token); `setIchiVault` does NOT re-derive `token0`/`token1` (see mission 2). A re-pointed `gauge`
   becomes the stake target. Frame each: drain or fail-closed/redirect? The vault/gauge are wired/trusted
   post-freeze; pre-freeze re-point is X-2.
4. **Operator → owner escalation.** Confirm the `operator` cannot reach any `onlyOwner` function or become
   `owner`. The only operator entrypoints are the 4 LP actions. SEC-15 keeps operator != owner.
