# Mission 4 — The privilege, redirect & wiring adversary

MISSION: 4 — access gates, the clone init-lock, the destination pins, and the build-phase wiring blast radius

## Who you are
You attack the access-control, destination, and configuration surface: the operator gate, the SEC-14 mastercopy
lock, the SEC-15 owner-recheck, the `setUp` guards, the recycle/divert destination pins, and the 7 `onlyOwner`
wiring setters. Your goal: ONE path where a non-operator drives a spend, the mastercopy is initializable, recycle/
divert value routes to an operator-chosen destination, or an owner re-point DRAINS.

## The claims you are attacking
- **Operator gate (`NotOperator`).** `creditFreeValue`, `recycle`, `divert` are `onlyOperator` (`:241`, `:263`,
  `:307`); the modifier checks `msg.sender == operator` (`:175-178`). **Find a non-operator path to a spend or a
  credit.** Confirm the operator CANNOT call any `onlyOwner` setter or the inherited `setAvatar`/`setTarget`.
- **Destination pins (no operator-supplied destination).** `recycle` mints to the Safe (the `ZipDepositModule`
  credits msg.sender = the Safe); `divert` credits `eePool.deposit(usdcAmount, warehouseSafe)` (`:330`) — the
  receiver is the set-once `warehouseSafe`, never operator-supplied. **Find a path where recycle/divert value
  reaches an operator-chosen destination.** The operator's only inputs are scalar amounts — confirm.
- **SEC-14 / SEC-15 / `setUp` guards.** Mastercopy locked (`MastercopyInitLock`); clone `setUp` once
  (`initializer`, `:138`). Eight addresses non-zero (`:150-156`), `owner != operator` (`:157`), validated before
  use, THEN `_transferOwnership` (`:171`). The X-Ray notes an `abi_length_mismatch` test. **Find a decode/
  validation-order bug** in the 8-address decode (`:139-148`).
- **X-1 / wiring blast radius.** 7 `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters: `setJuniorTrancheEngine` (`:186` — NOTE: does
  NOT sync avatar/target here, per the X-Ray, unlike the other engine modules), `setOperator` (SEC-15),
  `setZipDepositModule`, `setUsdc`, plus the Stream-2 trio `setNavOracle`/`setEePool`/`setWarehouseSafe`. **Pressure-
  test the highest-consequence ones:** re-pointing `warehouseSafe` (`:229`) changes WHO gets the senior shares a
  divert credits — a re-point to an attacker Safe would route divert value to the attacker. Re-pointing
  `navOracle` (`:215`) changes the SEC-09 hole read. Re-pointing `zipDepositModule`/`eePool` points the spend at
  an attacker contract holding a USDC approve. Frame each: drain or redirect-future/fail-closed?

## Specific attacks to run
1. **`setWarehouseSafe` → attacker.** `divert` credits `warehouseSafe` (`:330`). A Timelock re-point of
   `warehouseSafe` (`:229`) to an attacker address would route ALL future divert senior-shares to the attacker
   (the `BackingShortfall` still passes — the Safe's USDC did fall; only the SHARE receiver changed). Is this a
   DRAIN of future diverts (the shares represent the supplied USDC) or a governance redirect (the Timelock chose
   it)? This is the sharpest wiring question — work it: the divert SUPPLIES USDC and the receiver gets the
   claim-back rights. A hostile receiver gets the senior position. Rate against X-2 (it's owner-only, pre-freeze).
2. **`setNavOracle` → fake hole.** A re-pointed `navOracle` (`:215`) returns an attacker-chosen `provision()`. A
   huge fake hole would let `divert` over-supply the senior pool (within `freeValueAccrued`, which is the real
   ceiling). Is that a drain (no — the USDC goes to `warehouseSafe`, and is bounded by the credited budget) or a
   policy bypass? Rate: it's owner-only (X-2); the hard-backing + finite budget bound the damage.
3. **`setZipDepositModule`/`setEePool` → hostile spend target.** A re-pointed deposit/pool becomes the approve
   spender + call target. The approve is exact-`usdcAmount` and reset to 0; the deposit pulls from the Safe. A
   hostile target could take the approve. Drain (bounded to one call's `usdcAmount`, Timelock-chosen) or grief?
4. **Operator → owner escalation + `creditFreeValue` reach.** Confirm the operator cannot call any setter or
   become owner. The operator CAN call `creditFreeValue` unbounded (X-1, accepted) — confirm that's the only
   operator-reachable state write and it's bounded by hard-backing (mission 2).
