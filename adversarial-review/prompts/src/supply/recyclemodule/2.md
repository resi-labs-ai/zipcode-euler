# Mission 2 — The free-value conservation adversary (can the ledger conjure or overspend value?)

MISSION: 2 — two-layer enforcement (I-1/I-2): policy ceiling + hard backing

## Who you are
You attack the load-bearing free-value invariant: the ledger can be credited ONLY by `creditFreeValue`, debited
ONLY by `recycle`+`divert`, never overspent (layer a), AND the actual USDC moved is pulled from the Safe's REAL
balance so even an over-credited accumulator can't conjure value (layer b). Your goal: ONE path where a spend
exceeds `freeValueAccrued` without reverting, where the ledger and the Safe's real backing desync, or where value
is INVENTED rather than merely mis-routed.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (ledger conservation).** Credited only by `creditFreeValue` (`:241-245`, `freeValueAccrued += amount`),
  debited only by `_spendFreeValue` (`:249-254`, called by both `recycle` `:264` and `divert` `:320`), reverts
  `InsufficientFreeValue` on `amount > freeValueAccrued` (`:251`). Both sinks share ONE ledger. **Find any write
  of `freeValueAccrued` not matched to these paths**, an overspend that doesn't revert, or a path where `recycle`
  and `divert` don't share the same ledger (they must — a divert should reduce what recycle can spend).
- **I-2 (two-layer: policy ceiling + hard backing).** Layer (a): `_spendFreeValue` debits first (the policy gate).
  Layer (b): the USDC is pulled from the Safe by the `_exec` legs (`ZipDepositModule.deposit` /
  `eePool.deposit` do `safeTransferFrom(Safe,...)`), and `divert` ASSERTS `beforeUsdc - afterUsdc == usdcAmount`
  (`:332`, `BackingShortfall`). **Find a path where the ledger says a spend happened but the Safe's USDC didn't
  fall by the right amount** (layer-b break = invented value), or where `recycle` lacks an equivalent hard-backing
  check and could mint backed zipUSD without the Safe actually paying.
- **The X-1 residual (creditFreeValue unbounded).** `creditFreeValue` (`:241`) is unbounded/operator-trusted. The
  X-Ray: an over-credit can MIS-ROUTE *realized* free value, never INVENT it (layer b bounds it). **Confirm an
  over-credit cannot escalate past mis-routing** — i.e. crediting `$1M` when only `$100` is real lets the operator
  call `recycle($1M)`, but the deposit pulls `$1M` from the Safe (which doesn't have it) → the deposit reverts
  (layer b). Confirm the Safe's real balance is the true ceiling.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Recycle hard-backing.** `recycle` (`:263`) debits first (`:264`) then `ZipDepositModule.deposit(usdcAmount)`
   (`:268`). Unlike `divert`, recycle has NO explicit `BackingShortfall` check — it relies on the deposit's
   `safeTransferFrom(Safe, usdcAmount)` reverting if the Safe is short. **Confirm `ZipDepositModule.deposit`
   actually pulls exactly `usdcAmount` from the Safe** (read `contracts/src/supply/ZipDepositModule.sol`) — if it
   pulled from somewhere else, or minted without pulling, recycle could mint backed zipUSD without backing. Trace
   the deposit's transfer-from.
2. **Overspend across the two sinks.** Credit `$100`. `recycle($60)` → ledger `$40`. `divert($50)` → should
   revert `InsufficientFreeValue` (`$50 > $40`). Confirm the shared ledger blocks the combined overspend (the
   X-Ray's `test_divert_then_recycle_share_one_ledger`). Find any ordering where the two sinks each see the full
   `$100`.
3. **The decrement-vs-revert window.** `recycle` debits (`:264`) BEFORE the deposit (`:268`). If the deposit
   reverts (Safe short), the whole tx rolls back — restoring the ledger. Confirm a failed recycle leaves
   `freeValueAccrued` unchanged (atomic). The X-Ray's `test_recycle_overspend_leaves_accrued_and_callcount_
   unchanged` covers the overspend case; confirm the deposit-revert case too.
4. **Backing-shortfall arithmetic.** `divert`'s `beforeUsdc - IERC20(usdc).balanceOf(safe) != usdcAmount` (`:332`)
   — confirm this catches a fee-on-transfer USDC (the Safe falls by MORE than usdcAmount → `!=` → revert) and a
   no-op pool (falls by 0). Could a token that REBASES between `beforeUsdc` (`:328`) and the post-read make the
   equality spuriously pass/fail? USDC doesn't rebase; confirm the wired asset.
