# Mission 2 — The allowance & reentrancy adversary (does the Gate fence hold?)

MISSION: 2 — exact-amount allowances, the Gate fence, reentrancy, on-behalf NAV-proportional mint

## Who you are
You attack the trust boundary between the module and the two externals it calls (the EE pool, the Gate). The
module approves EXACTLY what each callout needs and (for zip→gate) resets to 0; it guards both callouts with
`nonReentrant`; and the zap fences the Gate so the Gate cannot pull more than `zipAmount`. Your goal: find
ONE path where an allowance survives and can be drained, where reentrancy bypasses the guard, or where the
on-behalf mint mis-attributes shares/value.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-7 (exact-amount allowances; zip→gate reset to 0).** The zap `forceApprove(gate, zipAmount)` then, after
  `depositFor`, `forceApprove(gate, 0)`. **Confirm the reset always runs** (even if the Gate pulled the full
  amount — a leftover non-zero allowance to the Gate is a standing pull right). The deposit path
  `forceApprove(eePool, usdcIn)` is exact-amount and (per I-14) settles to 0 because the EE pulls it all —
  confirm that residual is provably 0, not assumed.
- **I-6 (reentrancy-guarded on both callouts).** Both the EE `deposit` and the Gate `depositFor` are behind
  `nonReentrant`. **Find a reentrant path:** a malicious EE pool or Gate (or a token hook) that re-enters
  `deposit`/`zap` during the callout. Confirm the guard covers BOTH entrypoints and the whole body (not just
  a sub-section).
- **I-3 (zap on-behalf + NAV-proportional).** szipUSD is minted to `msg.sender` (not the module), shares =
  value/navEntry rounded DOWN. Confirm the on-behalf recipient is the caller and the round-down is against
  the depositor (never over-mints szipUSD).
- **I-2 (deposit value-1:1 + warehouse custody).** `deposit` mints `usdcIn * scaleUp` zipUSD to the caller,
  EE shares to `warehouseSafe`, share-count-agnostic. Confirm the 1:1-by-value and that the share receiver is
  always the warehouse.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Surviving Gate allowance.** Construct a Gate that pulls only PART of `zipAmount`. Does the
   `forceApprove(gate, 0)` still run afterward (resetting the leftover allowance)? If the reset is
   conditional or skipped on a partial pull, the Gate retains a standing allowance to the module's transient
   zipUSD on the next zap — a drain seam. (Note the `ResidualBalance` delta check from Mission 1 should also
   revert a partial pull — confirm the two defenses agree.)
2. **EE→eePool allowance residual.** The X-Ray documents an intentional asymmetry: the zap does NOT reset the
   USDC→eePool approval, arguing the exact-amount approval is fully pulled so the residual is always 0.
   **Verify that:** is the approval EXACTLY `usdcIn` and is `usdcIn` exactly what `eePool.deposit` pulls? A
   pool that pulls less would leave a USDC allowance — confirm I-14's claim that it settles to 0 holds for
   any EE behavior, or flag it.
3. **Reentrancy via EE / Gate / token hook.** Make the EE pool or Gate re-enter `zap`/`deposit` mid-callout.
   Confirm `nonReentrant` reverts it. Check whether USDC or zipUSD (any ERC-777-style hook, or a malicious
   token in a re-pointed wiring) could re-enter during a transfer.
4. **On-behalf mis-attribution.** Confirm `depositFor(zipUSD, zipAmount, msg.sender)` mints szipUSD to the
   CALLER, not the module or a fixed address. Confirm shares round DOWN (value/navEntry) so a depositor never
   gets more szipUSD than their value warrants (which would dilute existing holders).
5. **Capacity-bounded mint interaction.** Over-capacity `mint` reverts `E_CapacityReached` (not Panic) and
   rolls back the pulled USDC. Confirm the module pre-multiplies nothing into a `uint128` overflow before the
   capacity check (the overflow boundary must revert capacity, not Panic — cross-ref Mission 3).
