# Mission 4 — The wiring adversary (setTokens scaleUp re-derive, controllers, ctor)

MISSION: 4 — the four Timelock wiring setters, scaleUp re-derivation, ctor zero-guards

## Who you are
You attack the build-phase mutable wiring: the four `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters and the constructor.
Wiring is re-pointable until the pre-prod freeze (the subsystem residual, X-3) — but `setTokens` re-derives
the par `scaleUp` (the source for EVERY fill), and a setter missing its auth/zero-guard, or a ctor that
admits a degenerate decimals pair, mis-pars every redemption without any malicious flow. Your goal: find a
setter or ctor path that corrupts `scaleUp`, the controllers, or the token wiring such that fills mis-par or
the access gates fail open.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-14 (`setTokens` — onlyOwner + zero + `DecimalsTooFew` + `scaleUp` re-derive).** `setTokens(zipUSD_,
  usdc_)` re-derives `scaleUp` from the NEW tokens' `decimals()` and carries its own `DecimalsTooFew` guard.
  It is the par-scale source for every fill. **Find a path where the re-derive is stale (uses cached
  decimals), where the `DecimalsTooFew` guard is missing on the setter (vs the ctor), or where a re-point
  mis-pars in-flight pending** (a re-point mid-redemption changes `scaleUp` under an open request — does the
  whole-unit guard re-floor correctly?).
- **I-15 (`setController` — onlyOwner + zero + effect).** After re-pointing, the new controller can
  `settleEpoch` and the old reverts `NotController`. **Confirm the re-point actually switches the settle
  authority** (old controller fully loses it).
- **I-13 (`setRedeemController` discipline).** onlyOwner, zero-guarded, re-pointable — the gate on
  `requestRedeem`. A missing guard here lets an arbitrary address open a request (breaking the single-
  requester topology's trust anchor).
- **I-12 (ctor guards).** Zero ×3 (`zipUSD`/`usdc`/`controller`) revert; `DecimalsTooFew` when USDC finer;
  `scaleUp` derived correctly.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **scaleUp re-derive live.** Re-point `setTokens` from an 18/6 pair (`scaleUp = 1e12`) to an 8/6 pair
   (`scaleUp = 100`). Confirm `scaleUp` re-derives to 100 and the whole-unit guard (`shares % scaleUp == 0`)
   immediately floors at the new value (150 reverts, 200 escrows). Find any place a literal `1e12` is assumed
   that a re-point would leave stale — mis-paring every subsequent fill.
2. **DecimalsTooFew on the setter.** Confirm `setTokens` reverts `DecimalsTooFew` when the new USDC is finer
   than the new zipUSD (same guard as the ctor) — a missing guard would derive a negative exponent / underflow.
3. **Re-point under an open request.** With a pending request open, `setTokens` to a different-scale pair.
   Does the in-flight `pendingShares` (escrowed at the OLD scaleUp) get mis-pared at settle (new scaleUp)?
   This is the sharpest wiring finding — a scale change mid-redemption that breaks the par round-trip or
   solvency. Determine whether the pre-prod freeze discipline (X-3) is the only thing preventing it, or
   whether the code guards against a re-point with pending > 0.
4. **Controller auth swap.** Re-point `setController`: confirm the new controller settles and the old reverts
   `NotController`. Re-point `setRedeemController`: confirm the new one can `requestRedeem` and the old
   cannot. A missing zero-guard or auth gate on either is the recurring subsystem setter-gap class.
5. **ctor degeneracy.** Zero on any of the 3 args reverts; `DecimalsTooFew` when USDC finer; confirm
   `scaleUp` is derived (not hard-coded) at construction.
