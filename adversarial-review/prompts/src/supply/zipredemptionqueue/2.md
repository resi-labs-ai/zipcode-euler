# Mission 2 — The single-requester adversary (does the par-burn collapse stay sound?)

MISSION: 2 — settle/fill mechanics & the single-requester topology fence

## Who you are
You attack the design choice that makes the no-pro-rata par-burn core sound: **exactly one requester is ever
open**. `requestRedeem` is hard-gated to a single caller (the rq Safe); a second open requester must hard-
revert `MultipleRequesters`. If two requesters could ever be open simultaneously, the par-burn fill (which
has no pro-rata) would mis-attribute fills between them — a silent solvency/ownership bug. Your goal: find
ONE path where two requesters are open at once, or where the settle/fill mechanics mis-fill.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-5 (single-requester topology).** A second open requester reverts `MultipleRequesters`; the
  `pendingRequester` clears on full drain and re-opens for a new requester. **Find a path where a second
  requester opens before the first fully drains** — e.g. a partial-fill state where `pendingShares > 0` but
  the requester slot is wrongly considered clear, letting a second `requestRedeem` through and mixing two
  requesters' pending into one undifferentiated `totalPending` (the fill can't tell them apart).
- **I-4 (settle fills `min(available, pending)`, burns exactly filled).** `settleEpoch` fills `min(free USDC,
  pending/scaleUp)` at par, burns exactly `filledShares`, banks `claimableAssets`, bumps `reservedAssets`,
  clears `pendingRequester` on full drain. O(1), no time gate, never moves USDC out. **Find a fill that
  burns more/less than filled, or that fails to clear the requester on a full drain** (leaving a stale
  requester that blocks the next one, or clearing too early and letting a second in mid-fill).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Second-requester race.** With requester A partially filled (`pendingShares` remaining), attempt
   `requestRedeem` from requester B. Must revert `MultipleRequesters`. Find any state — post-partial-settle,
   post-claim-but-pre-drain — where `pendingRequester` is wrongly clear and B slips in. Then the fill from a
   later settle can't attribute USDC to A vs B correctly.
2. **Clear-and-reopen timing.** `pendingRequester` clears on FULL drain (`pendingShares == 0`). Confirm it
   clears exactly then — not on a partial fill (would let B in mid-A), not never (would block all future
   requesters). Test: A fully drains → B opens OK; A partially drains → B reverts.
3. **Fill bounds.** `min(free USDC, pending/scaleUp)`: full fill, partial fill (carries remainder),
   overfunded (caps at pending, excess survives), exact-capacity drain, zero-pending no-op, zero-liquidity
   no-op. Find a boundary where `filledShares` burned ≠ the USDC banked (par mismatch) or where overfunding
   double-fills.
4. **Burn exactness.** `settleEpoch` burns exactly `filledShares` zipUSD. A stray zipUSD donation must NOT
   inflate the burn (cross-ref Mission 3). Confirm the burn amount derives from the FILL, not the contract's
   zipUSD balance.
5. **No time gate / re-entrancy.** Settle has no time gate (on-demand) and is `controller`-only +
   `nonReentrant`. Confirm a re-entrant settle (via a malicious token during burn?) can't double-fill, and
   that the no-time-gate doesn't allow an interleaving that breaks the single-requester invariant.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_requestRedeem_secondRequester_reverts`, `_newRequester_ok_afterDrain` (I-5); `test_settle_full_fill`,
`_partial_fill_carries`, `_overfunded_caps_fill_excess_survives`, `_exact_capacity_drains`,
`_pending_zero_noop`, `_zero_liquidity_noop` (I-4). Confirming the single-requester fence + the fill mechanics
hold goes in the Summary. A finding must be a concrete two-requester-open state or a mis-fill (burn ≠ banked,
wrong clear timing) these tests don't model.
