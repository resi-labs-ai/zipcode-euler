# Mission 1 — The solvency adversary (can the sink ever pay out more than it received?)

MISSION: 1 — solvency (paid ≤ delivered), the balance invariants, par round-down

## Who you are
You attack the security-critical property: **solvency**. Cumulative USDC paid out must never exceed cumulative
USDC delivered, at every point in any request/deliver/settle/claim sequence. The par credits round DOWN
(sub-`scaleUp` dust locked), so paid ≤ delivered should hold by construction. Your goal: find ONE sequence
where the queue pays out (or makes claimable) more than was delivered, or where a rounding direction or an
accounting bump creates value.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (solvency: paid ≤ delivered).** Cumulative USDC out ≤ cumulative in (KR-5 #1). **Construct a
  sequence** — interleaved requests, deliveries (USDC funding), settles, partial fills, carry-forwards,
  claims — where the accounting lets `claimableAssets` or a `withdraw`/`redeem` exceed what was actually
  delivered. The par round-DOWN is the guarantee; find a path where it rounds UP or double-credits.
- **I-2 (the three balance invariants).** `reservedAssets ≤ balance`, `zipBalance ≥ totalPending`,
  `claimableAssets ≤ reservedAssets`. **Find a state transition that breaks any one** — e.g. a settle that
  bumps `reservedAssets` beyond the USDC balance, or a claim that drives `claimable > reserved`, or an escrow
  accounting where `zipBalance < totalPending` (under-escrowed pending).
- **I-3 (par round-trip is exact).** `requestRedeem(u·scaleUp)` → fund → claim == `u` USDC; round-DOWN never
  creates value, multi-epoch carry-forward sums to the exact total. **Find a multi-epoch carry where the
  rounding accumulates in the claimant's favor** (each partial fill rounding up by a dust that sums to >
  delivered).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Round-direction sweep.** Every par conversion (shares→assets at $1, assets→shares) must round DOWN
   against the claimant. Find the one conversion that rounds up or to-nearest — `filledShares` from
   `min(free USDC, pending/scaleUp)`, `claimableAssets` banked, `reservedAssets` bumped. A single round-up
   compounds across epochs into a solvency break.
2. **Double-credit on settle.** Drive multiple `settleEpoch()` calls across deliveries. Confirm each fill
   banks `claimableAssets` exactly once and burns exactly `filledShares`, and that an overfunded settle caps
   the fill (excess USDC survives as free balance, not double-counted). Find a path where the same pending is
   filled twice or banked twice.
3. **Carry-forward accumulation.** Multi-epoch: request 100·scaleUp, fund 30 USDC, settle (fill 30, carry
   70), fund 70, settle (fill 70). Confirm total claimable == 100 exactly. Try amounts that force rounding at
   each step (e.g. `scaleUp = 1e12`, deliver odd-USDC amounts) — does the sum stay ≤ delivered?
4. **Invariant break via donation.** A stray USDC donation raises `balance` but not `reservedAssets`; a stray
   zipUSD raises `zipBalance` but not `totalPending`. Confirm neither corrupts the invariants (cross-ref
   Mission 3's donation hygiene) — the invariants must hold under donations, not just clean flows.
5. **Reserved vs free.** `settleEpoch` bumps `reservedAssets` (banked for claimants) but the free balance
   (deliverable, not yet reserved) must stay ≥ 0. Find a settle that over-reserves (reserves USDC that isn't
   there) → `reserved > balance`.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`invariant_solvency_paid_le_delivered` (stateful, ghost accumulators) (I-1); `invariant_reserved_le_balance`,
`invariant_zipBalance_ge_totalPending`, `invariant_claimable_le_reserved` (I-2); `test_par_roundTrip_exact`,
`_carryForward_*` (multi-epoch, exact total) (I-3). Confirming solvency + the balance invariants + exact par
round-trip hold goes in the Summary. A finding must be a concrete paid-> delivered sequence, a broken balance
invariant, or a rounding-up these stateful invariants don't already cover — note the invariants run over
RANDOMIZED sequences, so a finding here must identify a transition the handler doesn't exercise.
