# Mission 3 — The extraction adversary (claim gates, SEC-12, donation hygiene, no-sweep)

MISSION: 3 — claim gates, SEC-12 canonical shares, reentrancy, donation hygiene, the no-fund-extraction surface

## Who you are
You attack the USDC-out and burn-seam surface: the only way value leaves is a requester's own
`withdraw`/`redeem`, there is NO sweep/rescue/pause, and stray donations must not corrupt accounting. Your
goal: find ONE path that extracts USDC the claimant isn't owed (over-claim, re-claim, non-requester claim, a
hidden sweep), an over-burn, or a donation that corrupts `totalPending`/the fill.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-7 (claim gates + SEC-12 canonical shares).** `withdraw`/`redeem` are requester-only; over-claim and
  re-claim revert (NOT silent-zero); sub-`scaleUp` `redeem` reverts `ZeroAssets`; `redeem` re-canonicalizes
  the emitted `shares = assets*scaleUp` (SEC-12). **Find an over-claim or re-claim that succeeds**, a
  non-requester claim that's allowed, or a `withdraw`/`redeem` parity mismatch (the two must pay identically).
- **I-9 (no fund-extraction surface — KR-2).** `settleEpoch` moves NO USDC out (only banks claimable); there
  is no `sweep`/`rescue`/`pause` selector on the ABI. **Find any function (or inherited `Ownable` power) that
  moves USDC out other than a requester's claim** — an owner-only rescue, a re-pointable wiring that redirects
  a payout, a selector the no-sweep ABI check misses.
- **I-8 (donation hygiene).** Stray zipUSD must not inflate `totalPending` or cause over-burn (settle burns
  exactly `filledShares`); stray USDC distributes at par with the excess surviving. **Find a donation that
  corrupts accounting** — a zipUSD donation that the settle burns (over-burn), or a USDC donation that
  becomes wrongly claimable.
- **I-10 (reentrancy-guarded claim).** `withdraw`/`redeem` are `nonReentrant` (effects-before-interaction).
  Find a reentrant claim (via a malicious USDC mock / token hook) that double-pays.
- **I-11 (real-ESynth burn seam).** Two REPAY rounds drop `totalSupply` by exactly `filledShares`, zero
  EulerEarn coupling. Confirm the burn uses no allowance/no capacity path and is bounded by the fill.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Over/re-claim.** Claim more than `maxWithdraw`/`claimableAssets` (must revert `InsufficientClaimable`);
   claim twice (the second must revert, NOT return silent-zero — a silent-zero would hide a logic bug and
   could be looped). Confirm `withdraw(assets)` and `redeem(shares)` pay identically (`_withdraw_redeem_parity`).
2. **Non-requester claim.** A caller that isn't the requester calls `withdraw`/`redeem` → must revert
   `NotAuthorized`. Confirm the `receiver` can differ from the requester but the AUTHORITY is the requester.
3. **SEC-12 canonical-shares.** `redeem` emits `shares == assets*scaleUp` (canonical, re-derived) — find a
   path where the emitted shares diverge from the assets paid (a 7540-accounting inconsistency a downstream
   indexer would mis-read), or where a sub-`scaleUp` redeem returns silent-zero instead of reverting
   `ZeroAssets`.
4. **No-sweep ABI.** Enumerate every external/public function + every inherited `Ownable` power. Confirm NONE
   moves USDC out except a requester claim. The fork test asserts no `sweep`/`rescue`/`pause` selector —
   find a differently-named function with the same effect (a wiring setter that redirects, an owner withdraw).
5. **Donation corruption.** Donate zipUSD: confirm `totalPending` unchanged and the next settle burns exactly
   `filledShares` (not the inflated balance). Donate USDC: confirm it's distributable at par and the excess
   survives as free balance (not auto-credited to the open requester beyond their pending).
