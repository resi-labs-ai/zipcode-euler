# Mission 2 — The coverage-accounting adversary (LP double-count / gross-parity / the double-squeeze)

MISSION: 2 — coverage = committedValue + pathLockedLpEquity, single-counted (I-4); gross invariant under rotation (I-8)

## Who you are
You attack the floor's coverage numerator: the claim that the fenced LP is counted IN PLACE and exactly ONCE
(not double), and that `grossBasketValue` is invariant under a main↔sidecar rotation (so the floor is a pure
"did the sidecar keep enough" check). The drill's #2 question. Your goal: ONE path where the LP is double-counted
(inflating coverage so a breaching release passes), or where a rotation changes `gross` (so the floor moves for a
reason other than the sidecar's holdings).

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-4 (coverage = committedValue + pathLockedLpEquity, single-counted).** `coverageValue` (`:287`) =
  `committedValue()` (sidecar liquid legs) + `pathLockedLpEquity()` (the fenced LP equity). `pathLockedLpEquity`
  is **main-only** (per the SEC-02 fix) so a sidecar LP isn't double-counted. **Find a path where the LP equity
  is counted twice** — e.g. an LP that lands in the sidecar getting summed into BOTH `committedValue` and
  `pathLockedLpEquity`, inflating coverage. The X-Ray says `test_SEC02_floor_breach_covered_flips_false` proves
  the PRE-fix double-count would have falsely reported `covered()`; confirm the fix holds and find any residual
  double-count (e.g. via the gauge-staked or escrow-collateralized LP states the oracle sums).
- **I-8 (gross invariant under rotation).** `grossBasketValue` (`:267`) sums BOTH Safes, so moving a whitelisted
  asset main↔sidecar leaves `gross` constant; `freeValue = gross - committed` (`:272`). **Find a rotation that
  changes `gross`** — e.g. a moved asset the oracle prices differently in the two Safes, an LP that's valued
  differently main-side vs sidecar-side, or a leg whose two-Safe sum isn't conserved. `testFuzz_parity_no_LP`
  proves `gross == committed + free` exactly for plain legs; the LP parity is "within 2 wei" (pro-rata floor) —
  probe whether that 2-wei slack can be amplified.
- **The double-squeeze (documented, fail-closed).** `covered()` docstring (`:323-329`): a farm-utility borrow
  against the fenced LP pushes BOTH sides the wrong way — the numerator drops (`pathLockedLpEquity` subtracts
  strike debt) AND the floor rises (the borrow draws senior cash → `maxWithdraw` falls → `illiquidSeniorValue`
  rises). The two do NOT cancel; it's self-DoS (the borrower freezes its own outflow, recovers on repay).
  **Confirm this is liveness-only (a footgun), never a solvency hole** — i.e. it makes `covered()` MORE
  conservative (false when it could be true), never the dangerous direction (true when it should be false).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **LP single-count across states.** `pathLockedLpEquity` is the fenced LP in every state (loose + gauge-staked
   + escrow-collateralized) net of strike debt, main-only. Trace whether any LP that is ALSO summed into
   `committedValue` (sidecar) could be added again here. The SEC-02 fix made `pathLockedLpEquity` main-only —
   confirm there's no state where an LP straddles both. (This is an oracle-internal property, X-2 — but the module
   USES `committedValue() + pathLockedLpEquity()`; confirm the sum can't double-count given the oracle's
   contract.)
2. **Gross-parity break hunt.** Construct a rotation of each of the 5 legs and confirm `gross` is unchanged
   (oracle sums both Safes). Then the LP: the X-Ray notes ≤2-wei pro-rata slack. Can repeated rotations accumulate
   the 2-wei drift into a meaningful gross change (which would let the floor drift)? Rate: 2 wei × realistic call
   counts is negligible, but confirm it can't compound into a coverage inflation.
3. **The double-squeeze direction.** Confirm a farm-utility borrow makes `covered()` return FALSE more readily
   (numerator down + floor up) — the SAFE direction (freezes outflow). Find any borrow/repay sequence where the
   two effects instead make `covered()` spuriously TRUE (under-protection). The docstring corrected an earlier
   "debt nets out" rationale that was wrong — verify the CURRENT reasoning (they don't cancel) is right.
4. **`lpBurnKeepsCovered` consistency (the seam to LpStrategyModule).** `lpBurnKeepsCovered(lpShares)` (`:339`)
   computes `coverageValue - lpShareValue(lpShares) >= requiredCommittedValue` (saturating, `:342-343`). Confirm
   the `lpShareValue` subtracted here matches the coverage the LP contributes (so the gate and the actual
   dissolution agree — this is the other half of LpStrategyModule mission 1). A mismatch lets a dissolution drop
   coverage below the floor.
