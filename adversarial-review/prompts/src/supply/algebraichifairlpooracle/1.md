# Mission 1 — The manipulation-resistance adversary (does spot leak into the "fair" price?)

MISSION: 1 — manipulation invariance & the TWAP keystone

## Who you are
You attack the contract's reason for existing: the claim that the LP fair value is **invariant under an
in-block swap** because reserves are reconstructed at the TWAP tick, not spot. Your goal: find ONE path
where a spot-manipulable quantity still reaches the price, or where the TWAP itself is movable cheaply
enough to matter. Over-valuing collateral (price too high) is the dangerous direction — an attacker who
inflates the LP price borrows more than the collateral is worth → bad debt.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1/I-2 (lib + oracle): manipulation invariance.** `fairReserves` values each vault position's `L` at
  the TWAP `sqrtP` (`L` moves only on vault mint/burn; the TWAP is a time-average) and adds idle balances
  raw. `_token0InQuote` values the volatile leg at the **same meanTick**. **Find any leg that uses the
  SPOT tick / spot reserves / a balance an attacker can move in-block.** Specifically scrutinize: (a) the
  idle-balance term (`balanceOf(vault)` for token0/token1 — added "raw"; can a donation/flash-transfer to
  the vault inflate it within the borrow tx?); (b) `totalSupply()` (the pro-rata denominator — can it be
  moved in-block via a vault deposit/withdraw to shift price-per-share?); (c) whether `meanTick` is truly
  the only tick used, or `getTotalAmounts()`/`globalState().tick` sneaks in anywhere.
- **X-2 (the documented residual): TWAP trust.** Manipulation invariance is only as strong as the Algebra
  plugin's TWAP. The lib does NOT check observation cardinality or staleness — it trusts `getTimepoints`.
  **Construct the cost-of-capital attack:** a 1h window (`twapWindow=3600` deployed) moved by a sustained
  multi-block reserve skew, or a pool with low observation cardinality where the "TWAP" collapses toward
  spot. Quantify: is the window/cardinality economically safe for a collateral oracle, and what does a
  sustained skew cost vs the borrow it unlocks?

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Idle-balance donation.** `fairReserves` adds `IICHIVault.balanceOf` idle token0/token1 as raw amounts.
   Can an attacker `transfer` token0/token1 directly to the vault (a donation) right before borrowing, so
   `tvlInQuote` rises with no real position change? Trace whether idle balances are part of the quoted TVL
   and whether the pro-rata (`/totalSupply`) neutralizes it (donation raises TVL but not supply → price per
   share rises → over-value). This is the classic LP-oracle donation seam — does the fair-reserves design
   actually close it, or only the *position* legs?
2. **Same-block supply shift.** `totalSupply()` is read live. Can a vault deposit/withdraw in the same tx
   (or a sandwich) move supply to shift the pro-rata? ICHI deposits are usually not attacker-controlled mid-
   borrow, but confirm the denominator can't be gamed.
3. **meanTick vs spot tick.** Read `_meanTick`: does it compute the arithmetic-mean tick over
   `[now-window, now]` via `getTimepoints`, or can a degenerate/length-issue make it fall back to the
   current tick? Confirm `_token0InQuote` uses `meanTick`, not `globalState().tick`. Any spot tick in the
   path breaks I-1.
4. **TWAP window/cardinality economics (X-2).** With a 1h window: how much sustained reserve displacement
   (and for how many blocks) moves the mean tick by X bps, and does the resulting LP over-valuation exceed
   the cost? Does the oracle/lib assert the plugin has enough observations to actually cover the window, or
   could a freshly-seeded / low-cardinality plugin return a "TWAP" that's really near-spot?
5. **Diff vs the methodology.** Compare against the Alpha-Homora fair-LP technique and UniV3 `consult`: does
   this implementation value `L` at the oracle price for BOTH positions (base + limit), and is the idle-
   balance handling consistent with "no spot-sensitive composition"? Flag any deviation.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_fork_manipulation_invariance` (300k-USDC swap → fair quote <1% while `getTotalAmounts` >2%),
`test_fork_fairReserves_match_getTotalAmounts_when_calm`, `test_fork_tvl_and_holder_value`. Confirming the
in-block-swap invariance holds goes in the Summary. A finding must be a concrete leak the keystone test
doesn't cover — most valuably the **idle-balance donation** path or a **realistic TWAP-window attack** with
numbers, not a restatement of the X-2 residual.
