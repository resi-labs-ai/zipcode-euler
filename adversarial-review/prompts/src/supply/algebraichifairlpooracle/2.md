# Mission 2 — The math-domain / rounding / overflow adversary

MISSION: 2 — tick-valuation domain, rounding direction, overflow at the boundary

## Who you are
You attack how the oracle *uses* the (trusted, faithful) vendored UniV3 math — not the math itself. Your
goal: a domain/rounding/branch-selection/overflow path that makes `_getQuote` **over-value** collateral
(round UP, overflow-wrap to a small divisor, mis-select a branch, or mis-handle decimals/address-ordering)
or silently mis-quote instead of reverting.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-5: rounds DOWN against the borrower.** `FullMath.mulDiv(tvlInQuote, inAmount, totalSupply)` truncates
  → under-values. **Find any arithmetic on the path to `tvlInQuote` that rounds UP** (the volatile-leg
  conversion, the reserve reconstruction, the meanTick rounding) so the composed TVL is biased high even
  though the final `mulDiv` floors.
- **I-8: `_token0InQuote` dual-branch correctness.** It mirrors `OracleLibrary.getQuoteAtTick` but takes a
  full `uint256` amount0 via `FullMath` (so a large vault never hits the `uint128` base-amount cap). The
  `sqrtP > type(uint128).max` branch (`:96-101`) uses the X128 form to avoid `sqrtP*sqrtP` overflow.
  **Find the input where the branch boundary mis-prices, overflows, or the two arms disagree** — and
  confirm the full-`uint256` path genuinely removes the `uint128` cap the library-review flagged (an
  over-cap amount must NOT silently wrap; `unchecked` math gives no revert).
- **I-4 (`_meanTick` rounds toward −∞).** The consult convention avoids an upward bias at negative ticks
  (HYDX/USDC live tick is negative). **Find a rounding-direction error** that biases the mean tick UP (→
  over-values the volatile leg).
- **Address-ordering / decimals.** `token0 < quote` is read at construction to select the ratio direction.
  **Confirm the direction is correct for the deployed single-sided-USDC vault and that token0/token1
  decimals are handled** (HYDX 18-dp, USDC 6-dp) — a decimals or ordering flip mis-scales TVL.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Rounding-direction sweep.** Walk every arithmetic step from `fairReserves` → `tvlInQuote`:
   `getAmountsForLiquidity` (does it floor or ceil amount0/amount1?), `_token0InQuote` (the `FullMath.mulDiv`
   direction + any `>> RESOLUTION`), the `amount1 + _token0InQuote(...)` sum. The safe invariant is "TVL
   never biased high." Find a step that rounds up or a `mulDivRoundingUp` where `mulDiv` (floor) is needed.
2. **The X128 branch boundary.** At `sqrtP` just above/below `type(uint128).max` (tick ~ where this flips):
   do the X192 (low) and X128 (high) arms return the same price (continuity)? Construct an amount0 large
   enough that the low arm's `sqrtP*sqrtP*amount0` would overflow — confirm the high arm is taken and is
   overflow-safe, and that neither arm `unchecked`-wraps to a tiny value (which would under-state the ratio
   and over-value... or over-state — work out the direction).
3. **uint128 cap removal.** The library-review flagged `getQuoteAtTick`'s `uint128` base cap as a silent-
   mis-quote risk. Confirm `_token0InQuote` takes `uint256 amount0` and uses `FullMath` so a vault TVL >
   uint128 is priced correctly, NOT wrapped. Find the amount0 magnitude where it would break, if any.
4. **meanTick negative-rounding.** Confirm `_meanTick` rounds toward −∞ on a negative remainder (matches
   `OracleLibrary.consult`); a round-toward-zero would bias the tick up at negative ticks → over-value the
   volatile (HYDX) leg. Diff the exact rounding expression against `consult`.
5. **Decimals / ordering.** Confirm `_token0InQuote` and the `quote=token1` assumption scale HYDX(18) →
   USDC(6) correctly (the quote leg is 6-dp). A missing decimal fold or an ordering assumption that breaks
   if a future vault has token0=USDC would mis-scale — note it even if the deployed vault is safe.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_getQuote_rounds_down_against_borrower` (forced-remainder floor), `test_token0InQuote_highSqrtP_branch`
(tick 600k, X128 arm reachable + equals reference; low arm cross-checked at tick 100k), and the math
primitives are vendored/faithful (confirmed — don't re-audit `ConcentratedLiquidity`). A finding must be a
concrete domain/rounding/branch error in the ORACLE's use of the math (e.g. an up-rounding step on the TVL
path, a branch-boundary discontinuity, a decimals/ordering flip), not a re-proof of the floor or the branch.
