# Mission 1 — The bracket adversary (can a sub-window spot move cheapen a mint or enrich an exit?)

MISSION: 1 — bracket asymmetry & the poke-spam-immune TWAP ring

## Who you are
You attack the oracle's reason for existing: the **bracket asymmetry** that makes the share price
unmanipulable within a window. `navEntry = max(spot, twap)` (issuance), `navExit = min(spot, twap)` (exit).
The design claim: a one-block spot spike can never produce a cheap mint (you always pay the higher of the
two) or a rich exit (you always receive the lower). Your goal: find ONE path where a sub-window spot move
leaks into `navEntry` cheaper, or into `navExit` richer, than the bracket allows — or where the TWAP itself
can be cheaply moved.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (bracket asymmetry).** Confirm `navEntry` is `max(spot, twap)` and `navExit` is `min(spot, twap)` —
  not a single value, not the wrong-way min/max. **Find any consumer-facing read that bypasses the bracket**
  and exposes raw `spotNavPerShare` to issuance or exit. Trace `spotNavPerShare` and `twapNavPerShare`: is
  the spot truly the in-block-manipulable one and the twap truly windowed? Could a degenerate TWAP (ring not
  yet full, freshly seeded) collapse `twap` toward `spot`, defeating the bracket on a fresh deploy?
- **I-2 (TWAP poke-spam immunity).** `poke()` is permissionless and advances the cumulative-TWAP integral +
  ring; `obsSpacing` caps ring consumption to 1 slot per spacing so ≥W of history always survives spam.
  **Attack the ring:** can a flood of pokes (or pokes timed at spacing boundaries) evict enough history that
  the effective TWAP window shrinks below W, pulling `twap` toward a recent manipulated spot? Check the
  wraparound (`CARDINALITY=65`), the `dt==0` no-op (same-block idempotence), and the within-spacing
  head-refresh-without-advance.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Wrong-way bracket.** Confirm `navEntry`/`navExit` map to `max`/`min` and not swapped; a single inverted
   comparison turns the entire defense into an attack amplifier (cheap mint + rich exit simultaneously).
2. **Spot leak into the bracketed read.** The bracket only helps if `spot` and `twap` are genuinely
   different sources. Trace `spotNavPerShare`: it composes `grossBasketValue / effectiveSupply` at CURRENT
   leg prices. If a leg price is itself an in-block-manipulable spot quantity (vs a pushed/TWAP mark), then
   BOTH spot and twap eventually absorb it and the bracket gives no protection. Identify which legs are
   spot-sensitive in-block and whether the bracket actually defends them (cross-ref Mission 2's leg analysis).
3. **Fresh-deploy / cold-ring collapse.** Before the ring has W of history, what does `twapNavPerShare`
   return? If it falls back toward spot or genesis, can an attacker mint/exit in that window at a manipulated
   spot? Confirm the genesis/cold-start path (cross-ref I-11) doesn't open a bracket-free window.
4. **Poke-spam window shrink.** Drive 200+ pokes (the test does this) but also try adversarial spacing:
   pokes at exactly `obsSpacing` boundaries to maximize slot consumption. Does ≥W of history provably
   survive, or can the integral be made to span < W? Quantify the worst-case effective window after spam.
5. **Integral accounting.** The accumulator is advanced FIRST on a price write (old spot booked before new
   prices apply) and on `poke`. Confirm no path books a NEW price into the OLD time interval (which would
   contaminate the TWAP with un-aged spot).
