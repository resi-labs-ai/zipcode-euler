# Mission 5 — The wiring adversary (SEC-10 LP-TWAP validation, all-setter auth, ctor)

MISSION: 5 — SEC-10 LP-TWAP window validation, the seven Timelock setters, ctor zero-guards, wiring residual

## Who you are
You attack the build-phase mutable wiring: the seven `onlyOwner` (Timelock) setters, the SEC-10 LP-TWAP
window validation, and the constructor. Wiring is re-pointable until the pre-prod freeze (the subsystem
residual, X-3) — but a setter that admits a degenerate config (an LP-TWAP window with no initialized plugin,
a setter missing its auth/zero-guard, a ctor that seeds a bad obs[0]) breaks the keystone oracle without any
malicious push. Your goal: find a setter or ctor path that corrupts wiring such that subsequent reads are
wrong or fail-open.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-8 (SEC-10 LP-TWAP window validation).** `setLpTwapWindow(>0)` requires `ichiVault` wired AND an
  initialized plugin, else `LpTwapPluginNotReady`; `(0)` is always a valid escape (use the pushed mark
  instead of the on-chain TWAP); the ready path reads through `fairReserves`. **Find a path where
  `setLpTwapWindow(>0)` succeeds without a ready plugin** (would later revert or mis-price every LP read), or
  where the `(0)` escape doesn't actually fall back cleanly.
- **I-15 (setter auth/zero on ALL wiring).** Every setter (`setShareToken`, `setLpPosition`,
  `setFarmUtilityLeg`, `setLpTwapWindow`, `setJuniorTrancheEngine`, `setDefaultCoordinator`,
  `setXAlphaRateOracle`) must be `onlyOwner` and zero-guard its non-optional args. **Find one setter missing
  its `onlyOwner` gate or its `ZeroAddress` guard** (the recurring subsystem setter-gap class). Note
  `setXAlphaRateOracle(0)` is a VALID owner unset (don't flag it); `setShareToken`/`setLpPosition` are
  set-once.
- **I-14 (ctor zero-guards + obs seed).** The ctor zero-guards the 7 identity addrs + `W` + `maxAge`, derives
  `obsSpacing`, and seeds `obs[0]`. Confirm a bad `W`/`maxAge` (zero, or `W` not covered by the ring) is
  rejected, and that `obs[0]` is seeded such that the TWAP doesn't read garbage before the first poke.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **LP-TWAP without a ready plugin.** Call `setLpTwapWindow(3600)` with `ichiVault` unwired or a pool whose
   plugin is uninitialized — confirm `LpTwapPluginNotReady`. Then confirm `setLpTwapWindow(0)` always
   succeeds and the LP leg falls back to the pushed mark / fair-reserves correctly (cross-ref I-7). A window
   set without a ready plugin would brick or mis-price every LP-containing NAV read.
2. **Setter auth sweep.** For each of the seven setters: a non-owner call must revert; a zero non-optional
   arg must revert `ZeroAddress`. Find the one that's missing a gate. Pay attention to `setFarmUtilityLeg`
   (TWO args — both zero-guarded?), `setJuniorTrancheEngine` / `setDefaultCoordinator` (ZeroAddress), and the
   isolation of `setLpTwapWindow`'s `onlyOwner` gate from its plugin validation.
3. **Re-point effect.** After a setter re-points a wiring slot, confirm the OLD wiring no longer influences a
   read (e.g. re-pointing `setXAlphaRateOracle` actually switches the rate source; re-pointing
   `setDefaultCoordinator` makes the new one the sole `writeProvision` caller and the old one revert).
4. **ctor degeneracy.** Zero identity addrs / `W` / `maxAge` revert. Construct a `W` larger than the ring can
   hold (`CARDINALITY * obsSpacing < W`?) — does the TWAP silently return < W of history (cross-ref Mission
   1's window-shrink)? Confirm `obs[0]` is seeded so a read before the first `poke` is well-defined, not
   garbage.
5. **Renounce interaction.** After ownership renounce, setters freeze (I-13). Confirm a renounce while a
   setter is mid-build can't leave the oracle in a half-wired, fail-open state (e.g. `lpTwapWindow` set but
   plugin never readied, now un-fixable).

`test_setShareToken_setOnce_and_auth`, `test_setLpPosition_setOnce` (I-15); `test_ctor_rejects_zero`,

Summary. A finding must be a concrete setter gap, an LP-TWAP-without-plugin admit, or a ctor degeneracy these
tests don't model. A bare "the Timelock can re-point wiring" restatement is the documented X-3 residual — INFO
unless it DRAINS or breaks an on-chain invariant.
