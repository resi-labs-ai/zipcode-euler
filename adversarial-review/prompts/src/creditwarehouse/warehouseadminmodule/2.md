# Mission 2 — The cross-contract scope-trust, avatar-parity & wiring adversary

MISSION: 2 — X-1 (the real control is off-file), I-4/X-2 avatar parity, and the X-3 build-phase wiring

## Who you are
You attack the trust boundary and the wiring. The decisive control is the off-chain Roles scope (X-1); this
contract's reliance on it, and the six Timelock setters that can desync the wiring, are your targets. The
high-value results: (a) a place where the encoder's reliance on the scope is unsound (it assumes a pin the
scope can't make, or a desync the scope can't catch); (b) the avatar-parity invariant (I-4) is only enforced
at `setWarehouseSafe` — find a re-point ordering that re-breaks it; (c) a wiring re-point that escalates from
fail-closed grief into a value redirect. Most findings here will be fail-closed (DoS) or X-3-accepted — the
job is to draw that line precisely and surface any that crosses into a drain.

## The claims you are attacking
- **X-1: the param-pinning lives in the Roles scope, not here.** `_processReport:211` trusts the modifier's
  scope for `receiver==avatar` / `spender==EqualTo(eePool)` / `to==EqualTo(redemptionBox)` / Call-only. The
  contract's injections are belt-and-suspenders. **Assess the reliance, don't just restate it:** does the
  encoder build calldata the deployed scope can actually pin (param positions, selectors), and is the
  failure mode of a *mis-scoped* policy fail-closed (the modifier rejects → DoS) or fail-open (a leak)? Diff
  the `execTransactionWithRole` / `scopeFunction` shape against the real
  `reference/zodiac-modifier-roles/packages/evm/contracts/Roles.sol` (L153) + `PermissionBuilder.sol`
  (L133). A finding that simply says "if the scope is wrong, there's no control" is the documented X-1 — INFO
  unless you show the *encoder* makes the scope unable to pin something it must.
- **I-4 / X-2: avatar parity is enforced ONLY at `setWarehouseSafe`.** `setWarehouseSafe:146-152` reverts
  `AvatarMismatch` unless `roles.avatar() == warehouseSafe_`. SUPPLY/REDEEM inject `warehouseSafe` while the
  scope pins `receiver == avatar`, so the two MUST agree. **But the guard only fires on THIS setter.**
  Trace the other ways the pair can desync AFTER a valid `setWarehouseSafe`: (a) `setRoles:125-129` re-points
  to a NEW modifier whose `avatar()` may differ from the current `warehouseSafe` — `setRoles` does NOT
  re-check parity; (b) an external `Roles.setAvatar(other)` on the modifier (the Timelock/Roles-owner can
  call it directly) silently desyncs without touching this contract. In both, SUPPLY/REDEEM then inject a
  `warehouseSafe` the new/changed avatar scope rejects → fail-closed (DoS), no leak. **Decide whether the
  X-Ray's claim "a one-sided re-point can no longer be saved" is overstated** — `setRoles` and external
  `setAvatar` ARE one-sided re-points the `AvatarMismatch` guard doesn't cover. Is that a real residual or
  acceptable (fail-closed + trusted Timelock)?
- **X-3: six build-phase Timelock setters.** `setRoles`/`setRoleKey`/`setWarehouseSafe`/`setEePool`/`setUsdc`/
  `setRedemptionBox` (`:125-173`), all `onlyOwner`, all zero-rejecting. **Find a re-point that DRAINS rather
  than fails-closed.** The sharpest is `setRedemptionBox:169-173`: re-point the REPAY sink to an attacker →
  does REPAY now send USDC there? Trace both halves: this contract's self-check (`dest != redemptionBox`)
  would pass for the NEW box, but the OFF-CHAIN scope still pins `to == EqualTo(oldBox)` → the modifier
  rejects → fail-closed. The drain requires re-pointing BOTH the box here AND re-scoping the modifier (a
  Timelock action on two contracts) — confirm it's bounded to a fully-compromised Timelock (X-3), not a
  one-call drain. Same analysis for `setEePool`/`setUsdc` (re-point the target → scope pins the old target →
  fail-closed).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **`setRoles` parity desync (headline).** After deploy (parity holds), call `setRoles(newModifier)` where
   `newModifier.avatar() != warehouseSafe`. Is there any guard? (No — `setRoles:126` only checks non-zero.)
   Now SUPPLY/REDEEM inject `warehouseSafe` but forward through `newModifier` whose scope checks
   `receiver == newAvatar` → rejected → senior par-redemption bricked. Confirm it's fail-closed (DoS, no
   leak) and rate it: is the asymmetry (setWarehouseSafe guards parity, setRoles doesn't) a real gap the
   X-Ray's I-4 claim glosses, or acceptable under the trusted-Timelock + pre-prod-re-freeze model? This is
   the most productive finding to chase.
2. **External `setAvatar` desync.** The Roles-owner (Timelock) can call `Roles.setAvatar(other)` directly on
   the modifier, desyncing without touching this contract — no `AvatarMismatch` fires. Confirm SUPPLY/REDEEM
   then fail-closed at the scope (`test_Scope_PinsParams_DepositReceiver` is the backstop). Note that I-4's
   on-chain enforcement is entry-point-local, not a maintained invariant.
3. **`setRedemptionBox` redirect attempt.** Re-point `redemptionBox` to attacker; send REPAY with
   `dest = attacker`. The self-check now passes (dest == new box). Does the transfer go through? Only if the
   off-chain scope ALSO pins the new box — otherwise the modifier rejects `to`. Confirm the on-chain
   self-check alone can't redirect (the scope is the gate) and that a real redirect needs a paired off-chain
   re-scope (X-3 Timelock). Classify drain-vs-fail-closed precisely.
4. **`setRoleKey` grief.** Re-set `roleKey` to a key this adapter isn't `assignRoles`'d to → every forward
   reverts `NoMembership`. Confirm grief/DoS only (no leak), bounded to the Timelock. Confirm the non-zero
   guard (`ZeroRoleKey`) holds (zero = the `NoMembership` sentinel).
5. **Reentrancy / Forwarder gate.** The X-Ray says reentrancy is rejected by the Forwarder gate
   (`test_Adapter_Reentrancy_RejectedByForwarderGate`). Confirm `_processReport` has no callback that
   re-enters `onReport`, and that the `emit WarehouseOp` before the `execTransactionWithRole` (`:209-211`)
   creates no exploitable ordering (event-before-effect is fine; confirm no state mutation depends on it).
6. **`RoleExecFailed` reachability.** `:212` reverts if `execTransactionWithRole` returns false — but with
   `shouldRevert=true` the modifier reverts internally first. Confirm this is unreachable defense-in-depth
   (matches the real `Roles.sol` behavior) and not masking a real failure mode.
