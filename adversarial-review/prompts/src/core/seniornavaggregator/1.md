# Mission 1 — The donation adversary (can a stray transfer move the aggregate, or does the per-silo math diverge from the freeze module?)

MISSION: 1 — donation immunity & the per-silo read math

## Who you are
You attack the contract's reason for existing: the **donation immunity** of the per-silo senior read. The
design claim is that the aggregate reads `convertToAssets(balanceOf(warehouseSafe))` and NEVER
`balanceOf(eePool)`, so a stray-USDC/shares donation to a pool address moves neither term and cannot inflate
or deflate the sum. The second claim is that the per-silo math is lifted VERBATIM from the audited freeze
module, so the aggregate agrees with it by construction. Your goal: find ONE path where the aggregate reads
something a donation moves, where a per-silo guard (`sa==0`, `free>=sa`) mis-handles, or where the math
DIVERGES from the verbatim freeze-module source.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (donation immunity).** Trace `_seniorValue` (`:52-57`) and `_illiquidValue` (`:61-68`). Confirm the
  share argument to `convertToAssets` is `balanceOf(warehouseSafe)` — the warehouse Safe's OWN position — and
  that nowhere does the contract read `balanceOf(eePool)`, `eePool.balance`, or any other quantity an outside
  transfer can bump. The donation-immunity contract is the interface's (`ISeniorPool:11-17`: both terms MUST
  be backed by real pool accounting). **Find any read that an outsider's donation can move** — a
  `balanceOf(pool)` slip, a wrong account passed to `convertToAssets`/`maxWithdraw`, or a leg that sums a
  donatable balance instead of the converted position.
- **I-4 (drained/empty silo contributes 0).** `_seniorValue:55` and `_illiquidValue:64` short-circuit
  `sa == 0 → 0` with NO special case for a drained/retired silo. Confirm a 0-share / 0-backing silo adds
  exactly 0 to the sum and never divides-by-zero or reverts. Check the per-silo getters' `eePool==0` guard
  (`:126,:135`) is the only special-case — that the donation-immune math itself needs none.
- **I-5 (illiquid = sa − free, with the verbatim guards).** `_illiquidValue` (`:61-68`) reads `free` ONLY
  when `sa != 0` (`:65`, after the `:64` short-circuit), then `free >= sa → 0` (`:66`, covering BOTH `==` and
  `>`), then `(sa − free) * 1e12` (`:67`). This is the un-drainable lent-out value. Confirm the `free >= sa`
  guard prevents the `sa − free` subtraction from ever underflowing, and that `free` is `maxWithdraw`
  (free/liquid) not the wrong account.
- **I-2 (Σ across silos).** Confirm the single-silo identity (`seniorBacking()` over one silo ==
  `convertToAssets(balanceOf(safe)) * 1e12`) and the two-silo sum (`:77-80` loop accumulates `+=` correctly,
  no overwrite, no skipped index).

## The verbatim diff (the highest-value check)
`_seniorValue`/`_illiquidValue` (`:52-68`) are claimed VERBATIM from
`contracts/src/supply/szipUSD/DurationFreezeModule.sol:302-309` (`illiquidSeniorValue`; guards at `:305-307`).
**Open that file and diff line-for-line:**
1. Is the share argument identical (`e.convertToAssets(e.balanceOf(warehouseSafe))`)?
2. Are BOTH guards present and in the same order (`sa==0 → 0` THEN `free >= sa → 0`)?
3. Is the scale identical (`* 1e12`, USDC 6→18-dp, applied exactly once)?
4. Is `free` read AFTER the `sa==0` short-circuit (so a drained silo never touches `maxWithdraw`)?
A finding here is a CONCRETE divergence: a dropped guard, a flipped comparison (`free > sa` losing the `==`
case), a double-scale or no-scale, or a `free` read before the short-circuit. If they are identical, that is
the soundness result — say so.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The `balanceOf(eePool)` slip.** The single most dangerous bug. Confirm NO read anywhere takes the pool
   address as the `balanceOf` / value subject. If the contract read `balanceOf(eePool)` (≈0 for a pure
   allocator, AND donatable), the whole defense collapses. It does not — prove it by tracing every `balanceOf`
   call's argument.
2. **Wrong-account `convertToAssets` / `maxWithdraw`.** Both must key off `warehouseSafe` (the position
   holder). A path that converts the pool's or a stranger's shares would read a donatable quantity. Trace the
   `s.eePool` / `s.warehouseSafe` plumbing from `getSilo` (`:78,:89,:99,:125,:134`) into `_seniorValue`/
   `_illiquidValue` — does the right Safe address reach `balanceOf`?
3. **Guard order / underflow.** Force `sa < free` (a silo whose free liquidity exceeds its converted backing
   — possible mid-accrual): does `:66` catch it (`free >= sa → 0`) BEFORE the `sa − free` at `:67`? A flipped
   or missing guard underflows (Solidity 0.8 reverts — a fail-CLOSED but a DoS of the whole aggregate read).
4. **Scale sweep.** Confirm `* 1e12` appears once per leg (`:56`, `:67`) and the inputs are USDC 6-dp so the
   output is 18-dp USD. A double-scale (1e24) or missing scale (6-dp) mis-sizes every downstream
   collateralization ratio (cross-ref Mission 2).
5. **Sum integrity.** The loop (`:77-80`) accumulates `total += _seniorValue(...)`. Confirm no index is
   skipped, no value overwritten, and that an empty registry (`ids.length == 0`) returns 0 (not a revert).
