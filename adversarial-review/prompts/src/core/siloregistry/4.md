# Mission 4 — The owner-gate & controller-re-point adversary

MISSION: 4 — owner gates + the controller re-point + the un-seedable count/flag (I-9, I-10)

## Who you are
You attack the access-control surface and the build-phase wiring: the claim that all five governed functions
are `onlyOwner` (Timelock), that `setController` re-points the slot-accounting authority safely (zero-guarded),
and — the posture confirmation — that `lineCount`/`active` can NEVER be caller-seeded. Your goal: ONE governed
function reachable by a non-owner, a `setController` re-point that breaks an on-chain invariant (vs merely
changing who can move the count), or a path where admission lets a caller seed `lineCount`/`active`.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-9 (all owner gates).** The five governed functions must revert for a non-owner: `addSilo` (`:164`),
  `retireSilo` (`:209`), `setActive` (`:220`), `setCurrentSilo` (`:228`), `setController` (`:280`). The gate is
  OZ `Ownable`'s `onlyOwner` (`@openzeppelin/contracts/access/Ownable.sol`, the audited base — reverts
  `OwnableUnauthorizedAccount`), with the owner set to `msg.sender` at deploy (`:153`, `Ownable(msg.sender)`,
  the Timelock). **Confirm every one carries `onlyOwner` and none re-implements the check** (a re-implemented
  gate is the bug class to look for — there is none here; confirm the base's modifier is used).
- **I-10 (`setController` re-point + zero-guard).** `setController` (`:280`, onlyOwner) re-points the
  slot-accounting authority, reverting `ZeroAddress` (`:281`) on a zero, then `controller = controller_`
  (`:282`) + `emit WiringSet("controller", controller_)` (`:283`). The constructor also seeds `controller`
  (`:153-155`, NO zero-guard at construction — confirm that's intentional, a deploy-time value the Timelock
  re-points once CTR-03 is live). **Find a re-point that breaks an on-chain invariant** — not merely "the
  Timelock can change who moves the count."

## The posture confirmation: `lineCount`/`active` are un-seedable (the footgun the design closes)
This is the load-bearing structural claim. Confirm it by reading the types:
- `addSilo` takes a `SiloConfig` (`:98-108`) that is **addresses only** — it has NO `lineCount` and NO `active`
  field. A caller physically cannot pass them.
- The registry seeds `lineCount = 0` (`:195`) and `active = true` (`:196`) itself on the success path.
A caller-seeded `lineCount` would be a capacity-desync footgun (a silo admitted already "full," or with a
phantom slot count that the controller's increment/decrement can't reconcile). Confirm the `SiloConfig` /
`Silo` split (`:82-95` vs `:97-108`) is what closes this — the stored `Silo` carries the two registry-managed
fields, the input `SiloConfig` does not.

## The build-phase residual (pressure-test to INFO unless it drains)
The build-phase mutable `controller` slot is the documented subsystem residual, frozen pre-prod via the
immutable re-freeze (process, not code; X-Ray §5 + Verdict). A bare "the Timelock can re-point `controller`"
restatement is **INFO**. To rate higher you must show a re-point that breaks an ON-CHAIN invariant — and
trace what a `controller` re-point actually does: it changes WHO may call `incrementLineCount`/
`decrementLineCount`. A hostile/wrong `controller` could mis-move `lineCount` (a slot-count desync, a grief),
but it moves NO funds (pure catalog), cannot admit/retire silos (those are `onlyOwner`), and cannot break the
topology web. So the worst a re-point reaches is a count desync under a Timelock-chosen hostile dependency — a
governance/wiring residual, not a drain. Frame severity accordingly.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The five `onlyOwner` gates.** Confirm each of `addSilo`/`retireSilo`/`setActive`/`setCurrentSilo`/
   `setController` carries `onlyOwner` (modifier from the OZ base) and reverts for a non-owner. Confirm the
   count functions are `onlyController` (mission 2) NOT `onlyOwner` — the owner cannot move the count, and the
   controller cannot admit/retire silos. The two authorities are disjoint by design; confirm no function
   bridges them.
2. **`setController` zero-guard + the ctor gap.** `setController` reverts `ZeroAddress` (`:281`); the ctor
   (`:153-155`) does NOT zero-guard `controller_`. Confirm the ctor-seeded `controller` can be zero at deploy
   (benign — `onlyController` then reverts everything until the Timelock sets it via `setController`), so a
   zero-at-deploy `controller` fails closed (no count can move) rather than opening a hole.
3. **The un-seedable count/flag.** Confirm there is NO setter for `lineCount` or `active` other than the
   registry-managed paths (`addSilo` seeds, `incrementLineCount`/`decrementLineCount` move the count,
   `setActive`/`retireSilo` flip `active`). A caller cannot reach either field directly. Is `active` seedable
   to `false` at admission? (No — `addSilo` hard-codes `active = true` at `:196`.)
4. **Re-point breaks an invariant?** After `setController`, does any STORED state become inconsistent? (No —
   `controller` gates future count writes only; no silo record, no `lineCount`, no `currentSilo` changes on a
   re-point.) Confirm `WiringSet` (`:283`) is the only side effect.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
The five `test_*_onlyOwner` (`addSilo`/`retireSilo`/`setActive`/`setCurrentSilo`/`setController`),
`test_setController_repoints`, `_zero_reverts`. Confirming all five gates hold, `setController` is
zero-guarded, and `lineCount`/`active` are structurally un-seedable goes in your Summary. A finding must be a
concrete non-owner-reachable governed function / invariant-breaking re-point / caller-seedable-count path the
auth tests do NOT cover — a bare "the Timelock can re-point `controller`" is INFO (build-phase residual,
pre-prod re-freeze) unless you show it DRAINS rather than griefs the count.
