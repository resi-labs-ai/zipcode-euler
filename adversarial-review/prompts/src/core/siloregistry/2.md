# Mission 2 — The slot-accounting adversary (can the concurrent-line count desync?)

MISSION: 2 — slot accounting: cap, decrement symmetry, onlyController (I-4, I-5, I-6)

## Who you are
You attack the concurrent-line counter: the claim that `lineCount` can only be moved by the wired controller,
fail-closed, can never over-fill past `MAX_LINES_PER_SILO=28`, and can never underflow/leak below zero. Your
goal: ONE path where a non-controller moves the count, where a silo accepts a 29th concurrent line, or where a
decrement underflows or leaks a phantom slot.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-4 (slot cap).** `incrementLineCount` (`:240`, onlyController) reverts `SiloFull` at the cap
  (`:242`: `if (silos[siloId].lineCount >= MAX_LINES_PER_SILO) revert SiloFull(siloId)`), else `+= 1` (`:243`).
  `MAX_LINES_PER_SILO=28` (`:78`). **Find a path past 28** — an off-by-one (`>=` vs `>`), or a count moved by
  anything other than this guarded increment.
- **I-5 (slot accounting symmetry).** `decrementLineCount` (`:248`, onlyController) reverts
  `NoLinesToDecrement` at zero (`:250`: `if (silos[siloId].lineCount == 0) revert NoLinesToDecrement(siloId)`),
  else `-= 1` (`:251`). The zero-guard is the double-decrement leak protection — `lineCount` is `uint16`, so an
  unguarded `-= 1` at zero would underflow to 65535 (Solidity 0.8 would actually revert on the underflow, but
  the explicit guard makes it `NoLinesToDecrement` — confirm). **Find a decrement that underflows or that frees
  a slot the registry never counted.**
- **I-6 (slot accounting is `onlyController`).** Both mutators carry `onlyController` (`:240`, `:248`), the
  modifier at `:147-150` (`if (msg.sender != controller) revert NotController()`). **Confirm no other path
  moves `lineCount`** — not the owner, not a depositor, not a silo. The ONLY writers are these two
  controller-gated functions; admission seeds it to 0 (`:195`) and nothing else writes it.

## The fail-closed posture (confirm, don't re-report as a flaw)
The controller (CTR-03 / `ZipcodeController`) calls `incrementLineCount` as the **LAST write** after a
successful `openLine` — `ZipcodeController.sol:257-260` ("bump the registry slot count as the FINAL
statement"), so a reverted origination leaks no phantom count, and `decrementLineCount` on close
(`ZipcodeController.sol:308`). Confirm the registry side enforces this correctly (the guard is on the count
value, not on call ordering — the registry trusts the controller to call it last; the registry makes NO
callback into the controller, so the atomic origination's revert unwinds the increment cleanly).

## The cross-ticket capacity caveat (DOCUMENT, do not re-report as a registry flaw)
The NatSpec (`:52-59`) documents that the as-built `EulerVenueAdapter.closeLine` frees only the **supply**
queue, NOT the binding **withdraw-queue** slot — so a pool bricks at ~28 *lifetime* opens inside `acceptCap`
BEFORE this registry's `SiloFull` would ever trip. This is a **wiring dependency on CTR-04** (the
withdraw-queue reclaim), NOT a registry flaw: `decrementLineCount` is correct *registry* accounting and builds
standalone; the concurrent-capacity model is only fully sound once CTR-03 wires increment/decrement AND CTR-04
makes `closeLine` reclaim the withdraw-queue slot. Mention it as the documented residual; do not file it as a
registry bug (the registry's counter is correct).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The cap boundary.** `>= MAX_LINES_PER_SILO` (`:242`) — at `lineCount == 27` the increment to 28 passes; at
   `28` it reverts `SiloFull`. Confirm the 28th line is accepted and the 29th is rejected (no `>` off-by-one
   that would admit a 29th).
2. **The underflow guard.** `lineCount == 0` (`:250`) blocks the decrement BEFORE the `-= 1` (`:251`). Confirm a
   double-decrement (close called twice, or a close with no prior open) reverts `NoLinesToDecrement` rather than
   wrapping `uint16` to 65535 (which would forever free phantom slots / mask the real count).
3. **The authority.** `onlyController` (`:147-150`) — confirm a non-controller increment/decrement reverts
   `NotController`, and that `controller` is the SOLE writer of `lineCount` after admission seeds 0. Is there
   any owner path that touches `lineCount`? (No — the owner gates admission/lifecycle/`setController`, never the
   count itself.)
4. **`UnknownSilo` on the count fns.** Both increment/decrement check `silos[siloId].adapter == address(0)`
   first (`:241`, `:249`) — confirm a count op on an unknown silo reverts `UnknownSilo` rather than initializing
   a phantom record at `lineCount=1`.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_incrementToCap_thenSiloFull`, `test_decrement_freesRegistrySlot`, `_decrement_zeroCount_reverts`,
`test_incrementLineCount_onlyController`, `_decrementLineCount_onlyController`. Confirming the cap binds,
decrement is symmetric + underflow-guarded, and only the controller writes the count goes in your Summary. A
finding must be a concrete over-fill / underflow-leak / non-controller-write the slot tests do NOT cover — NOT
a restatement of the documented CTR-04 withdraw-queue capacity caveat (a wiring dependency, not a flaw).
