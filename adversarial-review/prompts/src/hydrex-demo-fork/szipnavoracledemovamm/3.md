# Mission 3 — The CRE-push / freshness / rate-seam adversary

MISSION: 3 — the leg-price push path, freshness, and the xAlpha-rate seam (diff vs the parent)

## Who you are
You attack the **oracle's inputs and gates**, not the basket math: the CRE leg-price push
(`_processReport`), the TWAP advance (`poke`), the freshness gate, the stale-revert behavior, and the
fork-specific `setXAlphaRateOracle` seam (the demo adds a HYDX/USD leg and depends on the Base
`SzAlphaRateOracle` for the xALPHA rate). Your goal: a malformed/stale/missing input that the gates should
reject but let through, or a fork-introduced gap in the leg set.

## The claims you are attacking
- **Push guards.** `_processReport` (reportType 7) enforces: deviation band, not-future, non-zero,
  valid-leg, length-match, forwarder-only. **Find a malformed push (wrong leg id, length mismatch, a value
  just inside the band, a not-future-boundary ts) that lands and corrupts a mark.**
- **Freshness fail-closed.** Issuance (`navEntry`) reverts on a stale leg or stale rate oracle; exit
  (`navExit`) prices off the last good mark (the §7 asymmetry). `fresh` requires **both** required legs
  within `maxAge`. **Confirm both required legs are actually checked, and find a leg that's required for
  pricing but NOT in the freshness set** (a mark that can go stale without tripping `fresh`).
- **The xAlpha-rate seam (fork-specific).** `setXAlphaRateOracle` must be wired to the Base
  `SzAlphaRateOracle` (8x-02) or `grossBasketValue` REVERTS on the sidecar's xALPHA leg (the fallback mock
  mirror has no `exchangeRate()`); `setXAlphaRateOracle(0)` = use the M1 stand-in. **Probe this seam: a
  half-wired or stale rate oracle, and whether issuance correctly reverts on a stale rate (vs silently
  pricing).**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Deviation band + the new HYDX/USD leg.** The demo prices HYDX (a pushed leg the prod parent's leg set
   may not have had in this shape). Confirm the HYDX/USD leg goes through the same deviation band /
   non-zero / not-future guards, and that adding it didn't shift leg indexing so a push routes to the wrong
   leg (length-match + valid-leg). Diff the leg enum/set against the parent.
2. **Freshness set vs pricing set.** Enumerate which legs `grossBasketValue` *prices off* vs which legs
   `fresh()` *checks*. If the LP leg (HYDX mark) or the xAlpha rate prices NAV but isn't in the `maxAge`
   freshness set, issuance could mint off a stale mark without `navEntry` reverting. The X-Ray claims
   `test_fresh_requires_both_legs_within_maxAge` — confirm "both" is the COMPLETE required set for the
   fork's legs, not the parent's.
3. **Stale-rate-oracle revert.** Confirm `navEntry` reverts when the wired `SzAlphaRateOracle` is stale
   (`test_navEntry_reverts_on_stale_rate_oracle`), and that `navExit` intentionally prices off the last
   good rate (asymmetry). Probe the `setXAlphaRateOracle(0)` M1-stand-in branch: does the stand-in path
   have an equivalent staleness/zero guard, or is it a silent-value hole?
4. **grossBasketValue revert on unwired rate.** The docs say grossBasketValue REVERTS if the xAlpha rate
   oracle is unwired (the fallback mock has no `exchangeRate()`). Confirm this is fail-closed (a DoS on the
   view until wired, not a silent zero) and that it can't be exploited to wedge the oracle mid-operation.
5. **poke / TWAP advance integrity.** `poke()` is permissionless (advances the TWAP). Can repeated/timed
   `poke` calls skew the TWAP accumulator (e.g. advance it during a manipulated reserve state to "bake in"
   a bad value), feeding mission-1's bracket attack? Confirm the accumulator math matches the parent's.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_push_deviation_band_rejects`, `_zeroPrice_`, `_futureTimestamp_`, `_invalidLeg_`, `_lengthMismatch_`,
`_non_forwarder_`, `_wrong_reportType_`, `test_fresh_requires_both_legs_within_maxAge`,
`test_navEntry_reverts_on_stale_leg` / `_on_stale_rate_oracle`. Confirming these goes in the Summary. A
finding must be a concrete gap — most valuably a **leg in the pricing set but missing from the freshness
set**, or a hole in the `setXAlphaRateOracle(0)` stand-in branch, that the ported suite doesn't cover.
