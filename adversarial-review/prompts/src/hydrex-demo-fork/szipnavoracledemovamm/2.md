# Mission 2 — The NAV-accounting / donation / provision adversary

MISSION: 2 — basket accounting, the raw-balance donation seam, and the provision (diff vs the parent)

## Who you are
You attack the **accounting**, not the LP price: the raw-`balanceOf` basket sum, the unbounded
`writeProvision`, the `(gross − provision)/supply` share formula, the genesis NAV, and — fork-specific —
whether **dropping the farm-utility leg** broke any conservation property the audited parent relied on.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (donation seam, prod-inherited):** NAV prices the RAW `balanceOf` of the engine Safes, so a direct
  transfer into a counted Safe moves NAV with no deposit; the ExitGate's denominator (out of scope) is the
  only tie-back. **Confirm this is inherited, not fork-introduced, and that the fork's Safe set / leg set
  doesn't widen it** (e.g. counts a Safe or token the parent didn't, or double-counts a leg).
- **X-1 (unbounded provision):** `writeProvision` accepts ANY value from the `DefaultCoordinator`; the
  impairment bound (down ≤ atRisk·(1−floor)) lives off-chain in the DC, and the oracle must never be wired
  before the DC exists (fail-closed until then). **Confirm DC-only gating and that a DC mis-write can't do
  worse than the prod parent's** (it's the same seam — verify the fork didn't loosen the writer gate).
- **NAV formula + genesis.** `spotNavPerShare == (gross − provision)·1e18 / effectiveSupply`, `GENESIS_NAV`
  at zero supply. **Find an underflow (provision > gross), a wrong `effectiveSupply`, or a genesis edge the
  fork's leg-set changes.**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The dropped farm-utility leg.** Prod's `grossBasketValue` summed an ICHI vault leg + a farm-utility
   escrow collateral leg; the demo has **neither the ICHI vault nor the farm-utility leg** (only the vAMM
   pair LP + the plain legs). Diff the gross sum: did dropping the farm-utility leg leave a dangling
   reference, a now-always-zero term, or break a cross-leg invariant (e.g. a committed/free split that
   assumed the farm-utility leg existed)? A leg the parent counted that the fork silently omits could
   *understate* NAV (or vice-versa).
2. **Raw-balance basket sum.** Enumerate which Safes/tokens `grossBasketValue` sums (`_bal·mark` for
   zip/usdc·1e12 / xa·rate·alphaUSD / hydx / oHydx + the vAMM LP). Confirm each is the engine's balance, no
   double-count (e.g. the vAMM LP tokens are not ALSO counted as a plain leg), and the donation seam is
   exactly the parent's (a transfer in moves NAV — inherited, bounded by the Gate denominator out of scope).
3. **Provision underflow / ordering.** `spotNavPerShare` subtracts `provision` from `gross`. If
   `provision > gross` (a large DC write, or gross collapsed via an LP/leg move), does it underflow/revert
   or floor at 0? The X-Ray says `testFuzz_spotNavFormula` floors at 0 — confirm, and check the
   `committedValue`/`freeValue`/`valueOf` views handle the floored/negative-equity state consistently.
4. **`writeProvision` gate + pre-wire fail-closed.** Confirm `writeProvision` is `defaultCoordinator`-only
   and that before `setDefaultCoordinator` is wired, provision can't be set by anyone (fail-closed). Diff
   the gate against the parent — the swap shouldn't have touched it; confirm.
5. **effectiveSupply / genesis.** At zero supply, `GENESIS_NAV`. Confirm `effectiveSupply` matches the
   share token the fork wires (`setShareToken`) and that a first-deposit / zero-supply transition can't
   misprice (the donation seam + genesis interplay — does a pre-supply donation skew the first mint?).

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_grossBasketValue_plain_legs`, `test_spotNavPerShare_genesis_and_priced` + `testFuzz_spotNavFormula`,
`test_writeProvision_only_defaultCoordinator` / `_subtracts_from_gross`. Confirming these goes in the
Summary. A finding must be a concrete accounting gap — most valuably a consequence of the **dropped
farm-utility leg** (a dangling/incoherent term vs the parent), or a provision/supply edge the fuzz misses.
