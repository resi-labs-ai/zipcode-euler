# Mission 1 — The config-pins / mint-authority / subtraction-thesis adversary

MISSION: 1 — config-pins / mint-authority / subtraction-thesis

## Who you are
You attack a contract that "has no logic to break." Your job is to falsify that claim three ways: prove a
config pin is wrong, prove the mint-authority handoff is incomplete, or prove a dangerous surface leaked
in despite the subtraction thesis. The real risk on Base is **unbacked minting**, and it is governed by
role wiring that lives in the deploy script, not this contract.

## The claims you are attacking
- **`decimals() == 18`:** must match the 964 side + the pools' S8 for cross-chain conservation.
  **Confirm the ctor passes 18 and the base honors it.**
- **No staking / redeem / precompile / `IXAlphaRate` surface (subtraction thesis):** the security thesis
  is *absence*. **Confirm nothing — no override, no extra function, no inherited surface — exposes a value
  or stake leg on Base.**
- **Mint/burn gated to the CCT pool role; `maxSupply = 0` (uncapped):** **Confirm mint authority is the
  pool only and the deployer is revoked; assess whether the uncapped supply is genuinely bounded by 964
  custody or whether a lane desync leaves nothing to cap unbacked mint.**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Ctor-arg correctness.** Diff against `BurnMintERC20.sol`: do `(name_, symbol_, 18, 0, 0)` map to
   `(name, symbol, decimals, maxSupply, preMint)` in that order? A transposed arg (e.g. decimals vs
   maxSupply) would be catastrophic and silent. Confirm position-by-position.
2. **Role handoff completeness (the prize).** In `DeploySzAlphaBridge.s.sol`, trace the mirror's roles:
   `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE` and CCIP admin start as the deployer. Confirm the script (a) grants MINTER/BURNER
   to the Base pool only, (b) transfers admin to the timelock/multisig, (c) **revokes the deployer's
   admin**. A missing revoke = the deployer can mint unbacked mirror tokens forever. Confirm each step
   exists and is ordered so no gap leaves the deployer able to re-grant after "revoke."
3. **Uncapped-supply risk.** `maxSupply = 0` means no local cap. The X-Ray says supply is bounded by 964
   lock/release custody via the lane. Construct the desync: if the burn/mint lane ever minted more on Base
   than was locked on 964 (a relayer/RMN failure, a wrong remote-pool config on the pool), is there ANY
   local backstop? Confirm the answer is "no, by design" and that the only defense is the lane + pool
   config (cross-ref the TokenPool mission) — and rate whether that's acceptable.
4. **Surface-leak check.** Grep the inherited `BurnMintERC20` surface: does it expose anything beyond
   mint/burn/transfer/role-admin that, on Base, could be abused (e.g. a permit with a wrong domain, a
   callback hook, an approval race)? The subtraction thesis claims none of the *staking* surface exists —
   confirm, and also confirm no *generic ERC20* footgun (infinite approval + pool interaction) was
   overlooked.
5. **Name/symbol trust.** `name_`/`symbol_` are deploy params. Confirm they can't collide with or
   impersonate the 964 token in a way that matters for any consumer, and that they're set by the trusted
   deployer (cosmetic, but note if user-facing).

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_mirror_decimalsAndNoStakeSurface` (decimals==18 + no stake/redeem/precompile/IXAlphaRate surface),
`test_mirror_mintBurnGatedToPool` (mint/burn gated to the MINTER/BURNER role). Confirming soundness goes
in the Summary. A finding must be a concrete config/role/leak gap NOT covered above — most likely in the
deploy-script role handoff (step 2), which this contract's own tests do not exercise.
