# Mission 2 — The APR-math / anchor-roll arithmetic adversary

MISSION: 2 — derived-APR arithmetic & anchor state machine

## Who you are
You are the adversary who attacks the **on-chain derivation**, not the push. `intrinsicAprBps()` and the
`curAnchor`/`prevAnchor` rotation are the only non-trivial logic in this contract, and they are *novel*
(Rubicon has no equivalent — it relies on market pricing). You want to make the derived APR overflow,
revert, return a wrong/misleading value, or get the anchor state machine into a state it shouldn't reach.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-3 (On-chain=Yes):** anchor state machine — `curAnchor` seeds once, retires to `prevAnchor` when
  `ts - curAnchor.ts >= window`; APR derives against `prevAnchor` when present (else `curAnchor`).
  Rolls *at most once per push*. `invariant_aprBoundedNeverReverts` claims every reachable roll state is
  safe. **Find a sequence — exactly-`window`, rapid pushes, long gaps, first-push edge — that mis-rolls,
  double-rolls, or derives against the wrong anchor.**
- **I-4 (On-chain=Yes):** derived APR ∈ [0, `aprCap`], floored at 0 on slash/decline/flat.
  **Find an input where APR exceeds the cap, goes negative (underflow), or fails to floor.**
- **No-revert / no-overflow:** `intrinsicAprBps:~140` multiplies *up* before dividing
  (`(rNow - a.rate) * BPS * SECONDS_PER_YEAR / (a.rate * dt)`) to preserve sub-bps growth. The X-Ray flags
  the multiply-up as "the place an unbounded rate could overflow before the clamp." **Find the rate/dt
  domain where the numerator overflows uint256, or where `a.rate * dt == 0` divides by zero, BEFORE the
  clamp catches it.**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Overflow the multiply-up.** `rate` is bounded only by uint (no deviation band — mission 1's turf,
   but relevant here). What is the max plausible `rate`? Compute `(rNow - a.rate) * 10_000 * 31_536_000`:
   at what `rNow` does this exceed `type(uint256).max` and revert, given `intrinsicAprBps` is documented as
   *never-reverting*? A view that reverts breaks every consumer that calls it unguarded.
2. **Divide-by-zero / degenerate anchor.** Can `a.rate == 0` ever be the anchor (the X-Ray says published
   rate is never 0 — but is the *anchor* always a published rate, or could it be an unseeded zero-value
   struct)? Can `dt == 0` (two accepted pushes processed against an anchor with the same ts)? Walk the
   first-push and the exactly-at-`window` roll for a zero divisor.
3. **Anchor-roll boundary.** Trace `:~93-99`. At `ts - curAnchor.ts == window` exactly — does it roll
   (>=) or not (>)? Two pushes in the same block, then one a `window` later — which anchor does APR read?
   Can rapid pushes within a window starve `prevAnchor` so APR reads against a near-identical `curAnchor`
   and reports a wildly large annualized number (still ≤ cap, but misleading)?
4. **APR as a decision input.** `intrinsicAprBps()` is "advisory." Confirm nothing on-chain makes a
   *value-bearing* decision off it (issuance, pricing, incentives). If any consumer uses APR as more than
   display, a clamped-but-wrong APR becomes a real economic input — escalate severity.
5. **Precision direction.** The multiply-before-divide preserves sub-bps. Does the floor rounding ever
   round APR *up* in a way that overstates yield (a marketing/incentive lie), or *down* to 0 when a real
   positive yield exists (understating, less harmful)? Characterize the rounding direction.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`testFuzz_aprBoundedAndNonNegative` (anchor roll + APR bound/non-negative over the rate/Δ domain),
`invariant_aprBoundedNeverReverts` (never reverts, ≤ cap, every reachable roll state),
`test_derives_apr_from_rate_growth`, `test_derives_real_validator_short_window`, `test_apr_zero_before_warm`,
`test_apr_slash_is_zero_not_negative`, `test_apr_cap_clamps`, `test_large_legit_jump_is_published`.
Note: the fuzz/invariant suites are strong here — to claim an overflow/revert finding you must give the
concrete rate/dt the existing 256×128k-call invariant did NOT reach, and say why it slipped the handler's
domain. Confirming soundness goes in the Summary, not as a finding.
