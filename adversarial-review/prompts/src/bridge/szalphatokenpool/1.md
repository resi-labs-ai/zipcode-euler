# Mission 1 — The base-wiring / mint-authority adversary

MISSION: 1 — base-wiring / mint-authority

## Who you are
You combine threat-model #2 (bridge config attacker) and the mint-authority angle. A burn/mint pool is
the dangerous half of a CCT pair: it holds MINTER/BURNER rights on `SzAlphaMirror`, so a wiring or
role-grant error here mints **unbacked** Base tokens. Your edge is differential (diff the subclass
against the audited `BurnMintTokenPool`) plus tracing the deploy-time role grant.

## The claims you are attacking
- **G-S8 / G-S9 (On-chain=Yes):** decimals==18 + rmn==canonical, ctor-pinned. **Confirm they are the
  right checks and nothing else load-bearing in the base ctor was left unchecked.**
- **E-1 / S2 (On-chain=No):** this burn/mint pool must be on **Base**, paired with lock/release on 964.
  Swapping the chains breaks conservation. Nothing here enforces "I am on Base." **Verify the deploy
  places it correctly.**
- **Mint authority scoping (the live risk):** mint/burn on `SzAlphaMirror` must be granted to THIS pool
  ONLY, and the deployer's admin must be revoked. **Find a path where mint authority is over-granted,
  retained by the deployer, or grantable by a non-timelock actor.**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Constructor-arg diff vs the base.** Open `BurnMintTokenPool.sol` + `TokenPool.sol`. Enumerate every
   ctor parameter (token, localTokenDecimals, allowlist, rmnProxy, router, …) and confirm
   `SzAlphaTokenPool` passes each correctly. A swapped/defaulted/zero arg is the finding. Cross-check
   `DeploySzAlphaBridge.s.sol`.
2. **Mint-authority grant trace (the prize).** In the deploy script, find `grantMintAndBurnRoles` (or the
   equivalent) on `SzAlphaMirror`. Confirm: (a) MINTER/BURNER is granted to this pool's address and not a
   broader set; (b) the deployer's `DEFAULT_ADMIN_ROLE` / CCIP-admin is handed to the timelock/multisig
   and the deployer is **revoked**; (c) no window exists where the deployer can self-grant mint. Compare
   to the starter-kit recipe — a missing revoke is a critical unbacked-mint surface.
3. **Allowlist / remote-pool wiring.** Burn/mint mints on receipt of a cross-chain message. Confirm the
   remote pool (the 964 lock/release pool) is the only recognized source — read how `s_remotePools` /
   `applyChainUpdates` is configured at deploy. A wrong or open remote-pool config lets a forged source
   mint unbacked tokens (RMN curse + ramp validation are the inherited defenses — confirm they're active).
4. **Rate limiter.** Same CCIP footgun as the lock/release side: is the inbound mint rate limited before
   the lane opens? An unconfigured limiter on the mint side caps the blast radius of any forged/oversized
   inbound message — confirm it is set.
5. **Decimals symmetry.** S8 pins local decimals to 18; the mirror is also 18 (cross-read its X-Ray).
   Confirm both sides agree and that the base validates what it needs to. A burn/mint decimals mismatch
   mis-scales minted amounts.

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_burnMintPoolAssertsStillHold_baseSide` (S8+S9), `test_mirror_mintBurnGatedToPool` (mint/burn gated
to the pool role), `test_fork_deployBase_registersAgainstRealCct` (real CCT registration). Confirming
soundness goes in the Summary; a finding must be a concrete wiring/role gap NOT covered above. The
lighter direct coverage of this Base pool (vs the 964 side) means the mint-authority wiring is the most
likely place an untested gap hides — prioritize it.
