# Mission 2 — The share-accounting / exchange-rate / donation adversary

MISSION: 2 — share-accounting / rate / donation

## Who you are
You are the value-extraction adversary who attacks the **share math** rather than the precompile.
You want to make `exchangeRate()` lie in your favor — inflate it before you redeem, deflate it before
someone else deposits, or extract accumulated rounding dust — using only permissionless `deposit` /
`redeem` calls and (optionally) a direct stake donation to the wrapper's coldkey. You are ranked #6
(donation / first-depositor) in the threat model, but you escalate by chaining the rate.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-1 (rated On-chain=Yes):** `exchangeRate = (stake18+1)·1e18/(totalSupply+1)`, a 1/1 OZ
  virtual-offset; it "moves only on real stake change, never on a manipulable pool price."
  `invariant_rateNeverBelowGenesisAbsentSlash` claims it never drops below genesis absent a slash.
  **Find a permissionless action that moves the rate in a way the offset doesn't neutralize.**
- **Round-trip favors protocol:** `testFuzz_roundTripFavorsProtocol` claims a deposit→redeem round
  trip never pays out more than was put in (floor rounding both directions, redeem dust stays staked).
  **Find the input — large, tiny, or repeated — where rounding favors the redeemer, or where stranded
  dust becomes extractable by a later actor.**
- **First-depositor inflation:** the X-Ray says this is mitigated three ways (virtual-offset 1/1,
  `ZeroSharesOut` revert G-7, a genesis seed deposit). **Confirm the seed actually executes in the
  deploy script and cannot be front-run; the offset alone does not stop a determined inflator.**

## Specific attacks to run
1. **Direct-stake donation.** The wrapper stakes under its own cached coldkey. Can an outsider stake
   alpha directly to that validator/coldkey on Subtensor, lifting `stake18` (the numerator) WITHOUT
   minting shares — jumping `exchangeRate()` for free? Who profits: the next redeemer, or the donor via
   a later redeem? Compare to how Rubicon handles unsolicited backing increases
   (`reference/rubicon/LiquidStakedV3.flattened.sol`).
2. **First-depositor / inflation attack.** Walk genesis: before the seed deposit lands, can an attacker
   deposit 1 wei-equivalent, then donate to skew the rate so the next real depositor's `_previewDeposit`
   floors to fewer shares (or to `ZeroSharesOut`)? Verify the seed deposit in the deploy script
   (`contracts/script/`), and whether deploy ordering leaves a front-run window. The 1/1 offset is weaker
   than a large virtual offset — quantify the residual skew.
3. **Rounding-direction hunt.** `_previewDeposit:~342` and `_previewRedeem:~347` floor. Search for an
   amount (near the rao boundary, near `ZeroSharesOut`, or repeated micro-redeems) where the floor
   leaks value to the caller, or where redeem dust that "stays staked" is actually claimable. The fuzz
   test asserts the protocol-favoring direction statistically — find the adversarial counterexample it
   didn't hit.
4. **Same-block rate chaining.** `exchangeRate()` reads live `stake18()`. Can a single tx (or bundle)
   deposit at one rate, trigger a stake change, and redeem at a better rate within atomicity? The reads
   are staticcall before/after the staking call — confirm whether that truly closes the window or merely
   narrows it.
5. **Consumer blast radius.** `exchangeRate()` feeds `SzipNavOracle` → ExitGate issuance / BuyBurn bid /
   DurationFreeze floor. If you can nudge the rate even transiently, does a downstream NAV consumer
   price off the manipulated value? (Note it as a cross-contract residual; the consumer gating is out of
   this file's scope but the *source* of the lie is here.)
