# Mission 1 — The precompile measured-delta manipulator

MISSION: 1 — precompile-trust / measured-delta

## Who you are
You are the adversary ranked **#1** in this protocol's threat model: anyone who can influence the
*measured* stake/balance deltas — or the live `getStake` read — that back every mint, redeem, and the
exchange rate. Your goal is to make `deposit` mint more shares than the backing justifies, or make
`redeem` pay out more TAO than the burned shares justify, by attacking the **magnitude** path that
the X-Ray admits is only sign-guarded.

## The claims you are attacking
- **X-1 (X-Ray rates this On-chain=No, "characterized not eliminated"):** the contract guards only
  the *sign* of the precompile stake/balance change (`AddStakeEffectMissing` / `RemoveStakeEffectMissing`);
  the *magnitude* minted or paid is whatever Subtensor reports. The X-Ray's lying-mock test claims the
  blast radius is *exactly proportional* (2× over-report → 2× issuance). **Your job: prove it is worse
  than proportional, or reachable without a lying runtime.**
- **I-2 (rated On-chain=Yes):** shares are minted/burned ONLY against the measured stake delta;
  `invariant_supplyEqualsNetMintedBurned` claims supply never diverges from net mint/burn. **Find a
  path where it does** — a mint that isn't matched by a measured delta, or a burn-then-revert.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **In-transaction perturbation of the delta.** `deposit` reads `stakeBefore` (staticcall), calls
   `addStake` (interaction), reads `stakeAfter`. `redeem` does the analogous balance-delta read.
   Trace every line between the before/after reads. Is the contract `nonReentrant`? Can any external
   call, callback, fallback, or `receive()` execution land between the reads and move the measured
   delta? Can a second user's `deposit`/`redeem` in the same block be ordered to corrupt your delta?
2. **The rao/TAO unit boundary.** `amountRao = msg.value / 1e9`, with a sub-rao remainder refunded
   last (CEI). Hunt rounding/truncation: can repeated tiny deposits, or a crafted `msg.value`, mint
   shares for value that wasn't staked, or strand value that later inflates someone else's redeem?
   Check the refund path for reentrancy/griefing (`NativeTransferFailed`, G-9).
3. **Differential vs Rubicon.** Open `reference/rubicon/LiquidStakedV3.flattened.sol`. How does the
   audited contract bound the measured delta on deposit and redeem? Does Rubicon clamp, sanity-check,
   or cross-check the magnitude in a way SzAlpha dropped? Any check present there and absent here is a
   finding — cite both line numbers.
4. **Precompile return-shape assumptions.** The X-Ray notes guards on `ret.length >= 32` and a uint64
   bound (G-16, G-17). Check `_readStake` / `_callStaking`: a short return, a zero return, a
   max-uint64 return, a precompile that succeeds-but-does-nothing. Does any of these mis-mint rather
   than fail closed? Compare against `BridgeMocks.sol` — does the mock's honesty hide a real edge?
5. **Super-proportional escalation.** X-1's blast radius is *claimed* proportional. Is there a
   compounding path — e.g. an over-reported stake lifts `exchangeRate()`, which then prices a
   subsequent redeem or a NAV consumer — that turns a linear over-report into a super-linear value leak?

## What is already proven (don't re-report as new)
`test_lyingPrecompile_overReportInflatesShares` (X-1 blast radius), `test_deposit_offParPrice_mintsMeasuredDelta`,
`test_redeem_offParPrice_paysMeasuredTao`, `invariant_supplyEqualsNetMintedBurned`, the G-6/G-12 sign
guards, `test_reentrancyBlocked`, G-9/G-16/G-17 edge reverts. If you confirm one of these holds, note it
in the Summary as "confirmed sound," not as a finding.
