# Mission 5 — The wiring & config adversary (F3 submitCap bound, setters, IRM/curator, wire-check, AmountCap, authority)

MISSION: 5 — submitCap bounded to the minted vault (I-4/F3), CTR-13 line IRM + curator fee (I-11),
`_assertWired` (I-12/W3), AmountCap encode (I-13), authority gates (I-14), build-phase setters (I-16),
`seniorPool()` (I-17)

## Who you are
You attack the configuration surface — the wiring discipline that IS the security model. The claims: the
curator `submitCap` is bounded to ONLY the freshly-minted vault (never a caller-supplied market, F3); the
line IRM + curator fee route correctly (CTR-13); the birth-time `_assertWired` (W3) proves the router resolves;
the AmountCap encode round-trips and rejects zero; the ~13 build-phase setters are all `onlyOwner` +
`ZeroAddress`-guarded (with the two intentional zero-as-"off" sentinels); the authority gates hold; and
`seniorPool()` returns the EE pool. Your goal: ONE setter that drains/breaks an invariant rather than merely
redirecting, a `submitCap` on a foreign market, a mis-encoded cap, or an authority gate that lets the wrong
caller in.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-4 (F3 — submitCap bounded to the minted vault).** Step 4 of `openLine` (`:353-354`):
  `eulerEarn.submitCap(IOZERC4626(evault), type(uint136).max)` then `acceptCap(evault)` — where `evault` is the
  LOCAL `createProxy` result (`:341-342`), NEVER a caller-supplied address. **Confirm `submitCap` can ONLY ever
  target the freshly-minted vault** — there is no `openLine` arg that flows into the `submitCap` target. A caller
  steering `submitCap` at an arbitrary market would let them onboard a hostile vault to the shared EE pool.
- **I-11 (CTR-13 line IRM + curator fee).** Step 3: `setInterestRateModel(irm)` (`:343`); `if (curatorSafe !=
  address(0)) setFeeReceiver(curatorSafe)` (`:347`) — routes the EVK interest-fee GOVERNOR share to the curator
  vault (Euler keeps only its protocol share, capped 50%); `address(0)` ⇒ skip (governor forfeits ⇒ 100% to
  Euler). **Confirm** the adapter is the vault's governor at this point (so `setFeeReceiver`/`setInterestRateModel`
  are authorized — it created the proxy `:342`), and the curator-fee sentinel is honored.
- **I-12 (W3 — `_assertWired`).** `_assertWired(router, collat, lienToken)` (`:387-390`) calls
  `EulerRouter(router).resolveOracle(1e18, collat, usdc)` and reverts `WireMismatch` if `rBase != lienToken ||
  rOracle != oracleRegistry` (`:389`). It is `virtual` and NOT caller-reachable (openLine builds the wiring) —
  reached only by the `MisWiringAdapter` harness override. **Confirm** it proves `collat → lienToken → registry`
  resolves AFTER the router freeze (`:371`), so a mis-wired line can't be opened.
- **I-13 (AmountCap encode).** `_toAmountCap(amount)` (`:659-674`): low 6 bits = exponent, high 10 = mantissa
  (×100); rounds UP (smallest representable cap ≥ `amount`, so a line capped exactly at the draw doesn't revert
  `SupplyCapExceeded`); `amount == 0` reverts `ZeroCap` (`:660`) — raw AmountCap 0 means UNLIMITED, the opposite
  of a zero cap. **Confirm** the round-trip and the zero-reject. Find an `amount` where the encode overflows the
  10-bit mantissa, rounds DOWN (capping below the draw), or returns a cap meaning "unlimited".
- **I-16 (build-phase setters).** The ~11 infra setters (`setController` `:185` … `setFarmUtilityAllocator`
  `:271`) are EACH `onlyOwner` + `ZeroAddress`-guarded + emit `WiringSet`. `setFarmUtilityVault` (`:256`) adds
  the CTR-07 hook guard (`:263-264`, `FarmUtilityHookBlocksReallocate`). `setFeeBps` (`:295`) caps at
  `MAX_FEE_BPS` (`:296`). `setAdminSafe` (`:280`) / `setCuratorSafe` (`:289`) DELIBERATELY omit the zero-guard
  (zero = "fee off" sentinel). **Confirm** each guard; then PRESSURE-TEST: which re-point DRAINS or breaks an
  invariant vs merely redirects future behavior?
- **I-14 (authority gates).** `onlyController` (`:145-148`, `NotController`) on the lifecycle; `UnknownLine` on
  unknown `lineRef`; `liquidate` reverts `NotImplemented` (`:650-652`, §4.4e). Confirm no lifecycle method is
  callable by a non-controller, and `liquidate` is an intentional stub.
- **I-17 (`seniorPool()`).** Returns `address(eulerEarn)` (`:522-524`) — the venue-agnostic admission surface the
  `SiloRegistry` checks (CTR-10b). Confirm it returns the EE pool directly.

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The F3 trace.** Confirm `submitCap`'s target (`:353`) is the local `evault` (`:341-342`), with NO data flow
   from any `openLine` arg (`lienId`/`lienToken`/`collateralAmount`). This is the single most important wiring
   bound — a caller-supplied `submitCap` market is a HIGH. Confirm it's structurally impossible.
2. **Setter blast-radius ranking.** For EACH owner setter, ask: does re-pointing it DRAIN value or break an
   on-chain invariant, or only redirect FUTURE behavior (build-phase residual, INFO)? The sharpest candidates:
   `setGatingHook` (`:220`) — re-point to a permissive hook that disarms per-line isolation (foreign accounts
   could draw)? `setErebor` (`:241`) — redirect future draws (but F2 still pins to the NEW erebor)? `setOracleRegistry`
   (`:213`) — does it affect ALREADY-FROZEN routers (no — they cached the OLD registry at `:338`/`:371`; only
   NEW lines read the new one)? Frame each: owner-only (Timelock), closed by the pre-prod re-freeze; a finding is
   INFO unless reachable by a non-owner OR it drains/breaks an invariant on EXISTING lines.
3. **The CTR-07 hook guard.** `setFarmUtilityVault` (`:256`) reads `hookConfig()` (`:263`) and reverts
   `FarmUtilityHookBlocksReallocate` if `hookedOps & REALLOC_OPS != 0` (`:264`) — refusing a vault whose hook
   would brick `fundFarmUtility`/`defundFarmUtility`. Confirm `REALLOC_OPS = DEPOSIT|MINT|WITHDRAW|REDEEM`
   (`:36`) matches the ops a reallocate leg invokes. The comment notes the Timelock could still RE-HOOK an
   already-wired vault later (a governed §17 invariant outside this adapter) — that's the KNOWN residual, not a
   vuln.
4. **AmountCap edges.** Run `_toAmountCap` (`:659-674`) on: `0` (→ `ZeroCap`), `1`, a value needing mantissa
   exactly 1023, a `type(uint256).max`. Confirm the loop finds the smallest exponent with `mantissa <= 1023`
   (`:663-669`), rounds UP (`:666`), and the defensive final revert (`:673`) is genuinely unreachable for any
   uint256. A round-DOWN bug would cap a line BELOW its draw (reverts `SupplyCapExceeded` at draw); a `0`-return
   would mean UNLIMITED.
5. **`setLineLimits` scope.** `setLineLimits` (`:393`) calls `setLTV` (`:397`) + `setCaps(0, _toAmountCap(cap))`
   (`:400`) on the EVK VAULT — confirm `supplyCap = 0` is the DELIBERATE "unlimited" (the line's risk bound is
   the borrowCap + LTV×mark, not a supply cap), and that this NEVER touches the EE market cap (which `closeLine`
   relies on being max→0-zeroable — see mission 4). Confirm `UnknownLine` (`:395`) guards a closed/unknown line.
