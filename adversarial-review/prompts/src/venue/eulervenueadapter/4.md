# Mission 4 — The queue-ceiling adversary (the 30-market wall + close discipline)

MISSION: 4 — close reclaims BOTH EE queue slots (I-6: SEC-06 supply + CTR-04 withdraw), close guards on
repayment + reclaims the lien (I-8)

## Who you are
You attack the protocol's known scaling wall: the `EulerEarn` `MAX_QUEUE_LENGTH = 30` ceiling. Every `openLine`
appends the new market to BOTH the supply queue (`:355-361`) and — via `submitCap`+`acceptCap` — a binding
withdraw-queue slot (`EulerEarn.sol:783-785`). The claim: `closeLine` reclaims BOTH slots so origination NEVER
permanently bricks at the cap, even across churn far past 30 lifetime lines. Your goal: ONE leaked slot (supply
OR withdraw) that survives a close — because a single leaked slot is UNRECOVERABLE and permanently bricks the
~30th lifetime origination.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-6a (SEC-06 — supply-queue prune).** `closeLine` rebuilds the supply queue into a `qlen-1` array, skipping
  `lineRef` by ADDRESS match (`:575-583`) — NOT by assuming it is the last entry (interleaved opens/closes move
  it). **Confirm the prune removes EXACTLY `lineRef` by address**, every surviving entry still has `cap != 0`
  (so EE's per-entry check passes), and the removed market carries no balance (the defund ran first, `:558-565`).
  Find a path where the prune misses `lineRef` (leaves it in the queue → monotonic growth → brick) or removes
  the wrong entry.
- **I-6b (CTR-04 — binding withdraw-queue reclaim).** The HARD cap that actually bricks origination fires on
  the WITHDRAW queue inside `_setCap` when a market is first enabled (`EulerEarn.sol:783-785`). `closeLine`
  reclaims it in three steps (`:599-617`): (1) `submitCap(lineRef, 0)` — a cap DECREASE applies IMMEDIATELY, no
  timelock (`EulerEarn.sol:298-299`), satisfying the removal guard's `cap == 0` (`:362`); (2) build `keepIndexes`
  = every withdraw-queue index whose market != `lineRef` by ADDRESS (`:603-612`); (3) `updateWithdrawQueue(keepIndexes)`
  (`:617`) — any index NOT listed is removed. **Confirm:** the cap is zeroable max→0 (openLine left it
  `type(uint136).max` `:353`, `setLineLimits` touches only the EVK vault's OWN caps `:400`, never the EE cap);
  `expectedSupplyAssets(lineRef) == previewRedeem(0) == 0` (the defund emptied it) so the removal SKIPS the EE
  timelock/`removableAt` sub-block (`EulerEarn.sol:362-371`) and `delete config[lineRef]` runs. Find a path
  where the cap ISN'T zero (timelock engages → close reverts → slot leaks), or the index match is wrong.
- **I-8 (close guards on repayment + reclaims the 1e18 lien).** `closeLine` reverts `LineNotRepaid` if
  `observeDebt(lineRef) != 0` (`:530`); then redeems ALL escrow shares to the controller via `evc.call`
  (`:534-540`, the adapter is `borrowAccount`'s operator). **Confirm:** a line with outstanding debt cannot
  close (no abandoning a vault with a live borrow), and the full lien is reclaimed to the controller (not
  stranded in the escrow). `observeDebt` (`:510`) reads `IEVault(lineRef).debtOf(borrowAccount)` and does NOT
  guard on `open` (readable post-close, L7/L8).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The leaked-slot brick.** The X-Ray's `test_CTR04_WithdrawQueueBricks_WithoutClose` proves a leaked withdraw
   slot bricks origination. Confirm BOTH reclaim paths fire in EVERY `closeLine`: the supply prune (`:583`) and
   the withdraw reclaim (`:617`). Find ANY closeLine path that skips one — e.g. a `lineBalance == 0` never-funded
   line (the defund no-ops `:559`, but does the queue reclaim STILL run? It must — confirm the prune/reclaim are
   NOT inside the `if (lineBalance != 0)` block). This is the headline hazard: a never-funded line that closes
   without reclaiming its slots would still consume a lifetime slot → brick.
2. **The address-match correctness.** Both the supply prune (`:580`) and the withdraw keep-list (`:606`, `:611`)
   match `lineRef` by ADDRESS, explicitly NOT assuming it's the last entry. Construct an interleaved sequence
   (open A, open B, close A) and confirm A is removed from the MIDDLE of both queues, B survives, and the arrays
   are sized right (`qlen-1`, `keepCount`). An off-by-one or wrong-index removal leaks A's slot or evicts B.
3. **The cap-zero precondition.** `submitCap(lineRef, 0)` (`:599`) MUST make the cap exactly 0 for the removal
   guard (`EulerEarn.sol:362`). Confirm `setLineLimits` (`:393`) never touched the EE cap (it calls
   `IEVault(lineRef).setCaps` `:400` — the EVK VAULT's caps, a different thing from the EE market cap). If some
   path left the EE cap non-zero or a pending cap exists, the removal guard fails and `updateWithdrawQueue`
   reverts → close reverts → slot leaks. Find such a path.
4. **The timelock-skip claim.** The reclaim relies on `expectedSupplyAssets(lineRef) == 0` (defund ran first) so
   the `removableAt`/timelock sub-block (`EulerEarn.sol:366-370`, inside `if (expectedSupplyAssets(id) != 0)`
   `:365`) is skipped. Confirm the defund (`:558-565`) genuinely empties the market to `previewRedeem(0) == 0`
   BEFORE the reclaim — a donated share or rounding dust that leaves `expectedSupplyAssets != 0` would engage the
   timelock and revert the close. (Tie to mission 3's donation-immunity: a full redeem `{assets:0}` sweeps even
   donated shares per `EulerEarn.sol:397-402` — confirm.)
5. **Repayment guard + lien reclaim.** Confirm `closeLine` with `observeDebt != 0` reverts `LineNotRepaid`
   (`:530`) — including the CTR-09 fee debt (the fee leg added to the line's debt must be repaid too). Confirm
   the escrow redeem (`:534-540`) routes the full `shares` to the `controller` and that the `evc.call` (4-arg,
   the adapter as operator) is the right authorization (a direct `redeem` would revert
   `E_InsufficientAllowance` since the shares are owned by `borrowAccount`).
