# Mission 2 — The draw adversary (F2 receiver pin + the CTR-09 fee leg)

MISSION: 2 — draw pinned to `erebor` (I-3/F2), the CTR-09 per-draw fee leg (I-10)

## Who you are
You attack the draw path: the claim that EVERY draw goes to the immutable Erebor off-ramp (F2), that the
optional CTR-09 fee leg is a SECOND borrow on the SAME account (financed by the line, never an unfunded credit),
that F2 is preserved even with the fee on, and that the fee is off on the disabled sentinels / dust / zeroed
bps. Your goal: ONE path where a draw credits a non-`erebor` receiver, where the fee leg leaks value (a
`borrow(0,..)`, a `FeeLevied(.., 0)`, a fee that isn't financed by the line's debt), or where the fee escapes
its cap.

## The claims you are attacking
- **I-3 (F2 — draw pinned to `erebor`).** `draw` (`:451`) reverts `BadReceiver` if `receiver != erebor`
  (`:455`), AND the principal borrow leg hardcodes `erebor` as the receiver (`:485`, `IBorrowing.borrow(amount,
  erebor)`) — NOT the `receiver` arg. **Confirm BOTH:** the arg is checked against `erebor` (`:455`) and the
  actual borrow credits the hardcoded `erebor` (`:485`), so even if the check were bypassed the funds still go
  to `erebor`. Find any path where USDC reaches an address other than `erebor` on a draw.
- **I-10 (the CTR-09 fee leg).** `fee = amount * feeBps / 10_000` (`:462`, round DOWN); `levyFee = adminSafe !=
  address(0) && fee != 0` (`:463`). When `levyFee`, a FOURTH EVC batch item appends a SECOND borrow on the SAME
  `borrowAccount` crediting `fee` to `adminSafe` (`:492-499`) — so the line's debt becomes `amount + fee`
  (financed BY the line). **Confirm:** (a) the fee leg's receiver is `adminSafe`, the principal leg's stays
  `erebor` (F2 preserved on the principal — `:485` vs `:497`); (b) `adminSafe == address(0)` (disabled
  sentinel) ⇒ no fee leg, no `FeeLevied`; (c) a dust draw where `fee == 0` ⇒ no `borrow(0,..)` leg, no
  `FeeLevied(.., 0)` (`:461`); (d) `feeBps == 0` ⇒ `fee == 0` ⇒ same. Find a path where the fee is levied
  WITHOUT being added to the line's debt (an unfinanced credit = a drain), or where `FeeLevied`/a zero-borrow
  leg fires on a dust/disabled draw.
- **The EVC batch shape.** Items (`:465-499`): (0) `enableController(borrowAccount, lineRef)` self-call
  (`:467-472`), (1) `enableCollateral(borrowAccount, L.collateralVault)` self-call (`:474-479`), (2)
  `borrow(amount, erebor)` on `lineRef` `onBehalfOf borrowAccount` (`:481-486`), (3, optional) the fee leg
  (`:492-499`). `evc.batch(items)` (`:503`). **Confirm** the `onBehalfOfAccount` is `address(0)` for the
  self-calls and `borrowAccount` for the borrows, that the adapter is the granted operator (so the deferred
  account-status check at batch end passes against the final `amount + fee` debt), and that two borrows on one
  account in one batch is mechanically valid (the status check runs ONCE at batch end).

## Specific attacks to run
1. **The double-pin.** Trace `erebor` through `draw`: the guard (`:455`) AND the hardcoded principal receiver
   (`:485`). Could the `receiver` arg ever reach the borrow? (It shouldn't — `:485` uses the storage `erebor`,
   not the arg.) Could a `setErebor` re-point (`:241`) between fund and draw send a draw somewhere unexpected?
   That's a Timelock act (build-phase residual) — frame severity: a re-point redirects FUTURE draws, not a
   current drain; INFO unless it's reachable by a non-owner.
2. **The fee-financing invariant.** The fee leg is a `borrow` (`:497`), so the `fee` USDC credited to `adminSafe`
   is BORROWED by the line (debt += fee). Confirm there is NO path where `adminSafe` is credited `fee` without a
   matching borrow (e.g. a `transfer` instead of a `borrow`) — that would be an unfinanced payout (a drain of EE
   liquidity). Confirm the fee is bounded: `feeBps <= MAX_FEE_BPS` (5%) is enforced at `setFeeBps` (`:296`), so
   `fee <= amount * 500 / 10_000` = 5% of the draw.
3. **The dust / disabled edges.** Construct: (a) `amount` so small `amount * feeBps / 10_000 == 0` — confirm
   `levyFee == false`, the batch is 3 items (`:465`), no fee leg, no event; (b) `adminSafe == 0` — same;
   (c) `feeBps == 0` — same. The X-Ray proves these (`_DustDraw_NoFeeLeg`, `_NoOp_WhenRecipientUnset`,
   `_NoOp_WhenFeeBpsZero`) — find an edge they miss (a rounding boundary where `fee` is 1 wei and the leg is
   appended for dust value — is a 1-wei fee leg harmful? It's still financed, so benign — confirm).
4. **F2 with the fee on.** The X-Ray's `test_CTR09_F2_PinIntactWithFeeOn` proves the principal still hits
   `erebor` when the fee leg is present. Confirm the fee leg (`:497`) credits `adminSafe` and ONLY the fee leg —
   the principal (`:485`) is untouched. Could the two legs' receivers be swapped by any input? (No — both are
   hardcoded storage reads, not args; confirm.)
5. **`UnknownLine` gate.** `draw` reverts `UnknownLine` if `!L.open` (`:453`). Confirm a closed/never-opened line
   can't draw.
